# Genomic Markers Associated with Drug-Resistant Falciparum Malaria in West Africa

**Authors:** A. N. Okonkwo, M.D., Ph.D.¹; F. Adeyemi, M.D.²; R. Diallo, M.D.³

¹ University College Hospital, Ibadan, Nigeria
² Lagos University Teaching Hospital, Nigeria
³ Institut Pasteur de Dakar, Senegal

**Journal of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene** | Vol. 89, No. 4 | August 2021

## Abstract

We conducted a multi-site genomic surveillance study of *Plasmodium falciparum* isolates collected from 1,240 paediatric patients across three West African clinical centres between 2017 and 2020. Whole-genome sequencing identified six previously uncharacterised single-nucleotide polymorphisms in the *pfkelch13* gene region, three of which were associated with delayed parasite clearance following artemisinin-based combination therapy.

The lead author, Dr. Amara N. Okonkwo, is a paediatric infectious diseases specialist at University College Hospital, Ibadan, where she runs the malaria genomics laboratory. This is her fourth peer-reviewed publication in this area.
