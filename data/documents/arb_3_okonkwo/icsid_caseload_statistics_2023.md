# ICSID Caseload — Statistics

## Special Issue 2023

**International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes**
**Washington, D.C. — Issue 2023-2**

*This issue presents the ICSID caseload statistics for the calendar year 2023, with comparative data for the preceding ten-year period. The data reflects cases registered through 31 December 2023 under the ICSID Convention and the Additional Facility Rules.*

---

## Executive Summary

In 2023, ICSID registered 56 new cases — 47 under the ICSID Convention and 9 under the Additional Facility Rules. This represents a modest decrease of 6.7 per cent from the 60 cases registered in 2022, but remains broadly consistent with the average annual registration volume of 58.4 cases for the five-year period 2019–2023.

Total cases administered by ICSID — including those registered in prior years and remaining pending at any point during 2023 — reached an all-time high of 354 at year-end. Forty-two cases were concluded during the year, by award, settlement, or discontinuance.

The 2023 caseload reflects continued geographic and sectoral diversification, with notable growth in registrations from sub-Saharan Africa (eight cases, against a ten-year annual average of 4.3) and from the renewable-energy and digital-infrastructure sectors.

### 4.2 Notes on Sectoral Trends

The 2023 caseload reflects a continued shift in the energy sector toward renewable rather than conventional power generation as the principal source of investment-treaty disputes. Of the twelve energy-sector cases registered in 2023, nine concerned solar, wind, or hybrid renewable projects; only three concerned conventional fossil-fuel power generation.

The mining caseload remains substantial, with a continuing focus on Latin American and African respondent States. Of the six mining-sector cases registered in 2023, four involved disputes over the suspension, revocation, or non-renewal of exploration or exploitation permits. The remaining two involved disputes over the application of resource-rent and royalty regimes.

Cases concerning digital infrastructure — particularly subsea cable systems, data centres, and telecommunications licensing — accounted for four of the 2023 registrations, up from one such case in 2018.

## Section 6 — Tribunal Composition

### 6.1 Arbitrator Appointments in 2023

A total of 168 arbitrator appointments were made in 2023 in connection with ICSID cases. Of these:

- 67 (39.9%) were made by the parties acting jointly or by the appointing party
- 56 (33.3%) were made by the co-arbitrators (typically with respect to the presiding arbitrator)
- 45 (26.8%) were made by the Chairman of the Administrative Council in default

## Section 7 — Annulment, Revision, and Post-Award Procedures

### 7.1 Annulment Proceedings Registered in 2023

Twelve annulment proceedings were registered in 2023 under Article 52 of the ICSID Convention. These concern eleven distinct underlying cases (one underlying case is the subject of two parallel annulment applications by different parties).

### 7.2 Annulment Decisions Rendered in 2023

Nine annulment decisions were rendered by ad hoc Committees in 2023. Of these:

- 6 (66.7%) rejected the application for annulment in its entirety
- 2 (22.2%) annulled the underlying award in part
- 1 (11.1%) annulled the underlying award in its entirety

### 7.3 Cumulative Annulment Statistics through 2023

A total of 128 annulment decisions have been rendered by ad hoc Committees since the Convention entered into force. Of these:

- 84 (65.6%) rejected the application
- 19 (14.8%) annulled in part
- 25 (19.5%) annulled in full

## Section 8 — Procedural Innovations and Reform

### 8.1 Implementation of the 2022 ICSID Arbitration Rules

The 2022 ICSID Arbitration Rules took effect on 1 July 2022. As of 31 December 2023, 87 cases had been registered under the 2022 Rules, of which 56 were registered in 2023.

Tribunals operating under the 2022 Rules have made increased use of the new procedural mechanisms, including in particular:

- Expedited procedures under Chapter XII (5 cases as of 31 December 2023)
- Bifurcation under Rule 44 (32 cases in which bifurcation was granted, out of 47 in which it was requested)
- Decisions on manifestly without legal merit objections under Rule 41 (8 such decisions rendered in 2023, all dismissing the objection)

### 8.2 Mediation Caseload

The ICSID Mediation Rules, adopted in 2022, have generated modest but growing activity. As of 31 December 2023, the Centre had administered 7 mediation proceedings, of which 4 were registered in 2023. Three of these resulted in settlement.

## Section 9 — Financial and Administrative Notes

### 9.1 Average Duration of Proceedings

The average duration of proceedings concluded by award in 2023 was 4.3 years from registration to dispatch of the award. This is broadly in line with the average for the five-year period 2019–2023 (4.4 years) and reflects continuing Centre and tribunal attention to procedural efficiency.

### 9.2 Costs

The aggregate amount of fees and expenses approved by the Centre in 2023 in connection with arbitrator services was USD 79.4 million, up from USD 67.2 million in 2022. The increase reflects both the growth in active caseload and an upward adjustment in the standard arbitrator fee scale that took effect on 1 January 2023.

### 9.3 Advance Payment Arrears

As at 31 December 2023, 18 cases had outstanding advance-payment arrears, of which 6 had been suspended pursuant to Administrative and Financial Regulation 14(3)(d). The Centre continues to monitor advance-payment arrears closely and to engage with parties to bring matters to compliance where possible.

## Section 10 — Methodological Notes

The data in this report is derived from the Centre's internal case management system and reflects information available to the Secretariat as of 31 December 2023. Cases may be reclassified upon the receipt of further information, and the figures presented here may, in future publications, be revised in minor respects.

Cases are counted by registration: a case that has been registered but later discontinued, settled, or otherwise concluded is counted as a registered case in the year of registration and as a concluded case in the year of conclusion. Cases involving multiple respondents are counted as a single case.

Sectoral classification follows the ICSID internal sectoral taxonomy, which is broadly aligned with the United Nations International Standard Industrial Classification (ISIC) Rev. 4. The Centre acknowledges that some cases involve investments across multiple sectors; in such cases, the primary sector is recorded.

Geographic classification of respondent States follows the World Bank country and lending groups taxonomy. Geographic classification of arbitrator nationality follows the arbitrator's principal nationality as recorded in the appointment.
