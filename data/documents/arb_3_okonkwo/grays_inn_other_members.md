# Gray's Inn Square Chambers

## Members

*Chambers website — Members directory*
*Last updated: 11 February 2026*

---

Gray's Inn Square Chambers is a specialist commercial set, founded in 1947, with particular strengths in international commercial litigation, public international law, energy and natural resources disputes, international arbitration, and complex regulatory work. We are one of the longest-established sets specialising in international commercial practice in London, and our members appear regularly before courts and tribunals in the United Kingdom and across more than thirty other jurisdictions.

This page lists our full members and door tenants. For details of our practice management team, mini-pupillage, pupillage applications, and chambers operations, please use the navigation menu above.

## Full Members — King's Counsel

### Sir James Hartwell KC

*Called 1982, Silk 2004*

Sir James is widely recognised as one of the leading commercial silks of his generation. His practice spans international commercial litigation, banking and financial services disputes, civil fraud, and international commercial arbitration. He has appeared in many of the leading commercial cases in the English courts over the past two decades, including the Court of Appeal proceedings in *Sokolov v. Lambeth Wharf Holdings* [2019] EWCA Civ 412 and the Supreme Court appeal in *Phoenix Resources Ltd v. Northumbria Investments plc* [2022] UKSC 8. He is currently instructed in a substantial LCIA arbitration arising out of a $1.4 billion structured-finance dispute between two Eastern European parties.

Sir James was knighted in the 2023 New Year Honours for services to international commercial law and to legal education in the Commonwealth.

### Patricia Allenby KC

*Called 1986, Silk 2007*

Ms. Allenby's practice focuses on civil fraud, asset tracing, and international commercial arbitration. She has particular experience in cases involving the British Virgin Islands, the Cayman Islands, Jersey, and Guernsey, and is regularly instructed in proceedings before the courts of those jurisdictions. She has acted as arbitrator in over forty institutional arbitrations under the LCIA, ICC, SCC, and HKIAC Rules, and is on the Singapore International Arbitration Centre's panel of arbitrators.

She is the Standing Counsel to the Government of the British Virgin Islands in civil-recovery matters and has been a member of the Bar Council's International Committee since 2018.

### Sir Roderick MacAllister KC

*Called 1979, Silk 2001*

Sir Roderick's practice covers public international law, international commercial arbitration, and energy and natural resources disputes. He has appeared in proceedings before the International Court of Justice (as junior counsel for the Republic of Botswana in the *Kasikili/Sedudu Island* case), the International Tribunal for the Law of the Sea, the Iran–United States Claims Tribunal, and the ICSID Annulment Committee in *Patrick Mitchell v. Democratic Republic of Congo*.

He sits as arbitrator under the rules of all the major institutions and is currently presiding over four investment-treaty arbitrations. He was knighted in 2018 for services to international law.

### Bernadette Otieno KC

*Called 1991, Silk 2012*

Ms. Otieno's practice covers commercial litigation, international arbitration, and public international law, with a particular focus on African energy and mining disputes. She has been counsel in numerous investment-treaty arbitrations against African respondent States, acting for both claimants and respondents.

She is a member of the editorial board of the *Journal of African International Arbitration* and a co-author of the chapter on Anglophone Africa in the most recent edition of *International Commercial Arbitration* (Born, Sixth Edition).

### Daniel Markham KC

*Called 1988, Silk 2009*

Mr. Markham's practice covers international commercial arbitration, energy disputes, and international investment law. He has appeared as counsel in over sixty international arbitrations and as arbitrator in over thirty. He is a former Vice President of the LCIA Court and currently sits on the ICC Commission on Arbitration and ADR.

His current matters include the underlying proceedings in *Northvale Petroleum International v. Republic of [redacted]* (ICSID Case No. ARB/22/41) and a major LCIA arbitration arising out of a $3.2 billion gas-pipeline construction project in Central Asia.

## Pupillage

Chambers offers up to three twelve-month pupillage places each year, with a primary focus on candidates intending to develop practices in international commercial arbitration, commercial litigation, and public international law. Applications are made through the Pupillage Gateway in accordance with Bar Standards Board guidelines.

Pupils receive a Pupillage Award of £85,000 (twelve months) and are guaranteed first six months of guided pupillage with a designated pupil supervisor. Tenancy decisions are made in or around month nine of the second six.

We are committed to recruiting from the widest possible pool of candidates. We are signatories to the Bar Council's Race at the Bar Action Plan and the Disability Diversity at the Bar Action Plan, and we run a structured mini-pupillage programme that prioritises candidates from groups underrepresented at the commercial Bar.

---

## Mini-Pupillage

Mini-pupillage is open to current law students (graduate and undergraduate), GDL students, and recent graduates considering the commercial Bar. The mini-pupillage programme runs in three week-long sessions per year — typically in March, July, and December — and accepts up to eight candidates per session.

Applications for mini-pupillage are made online through our website. The application deadline for the July 2026 session is 14 April 2026.
