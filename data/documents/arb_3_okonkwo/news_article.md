# Okonkwo chairs landmark West African port award; sights set on AfCFTA disputes

*African Law & Business — 28 November 2023*
*By Tunde Adebayo, Senior Reporter*

A three-member ICC tribunal chaired by Dr Amara Okonkwo has handed down what practitioners are already calling one of the largest concession awards involving a sub-Saharan African state in the past decade, ordering a coastal republic to pay EUR 418.9 million to a Dutch-incorporated infrastructure consortium over the suspension of exclusive berthing rights at a deepwater container port.

The award, dated 14 November and seated in Paris, follows nearly two years of proceedings and a ten-day hearing held in May. Although the parties and the respondent state remain confidential under ICC practice, three sources familiar with the matter confirmed the principal figures and the composition of the tribunal, which also included Henri Lagarde of the Paris Bar and Sipho Dlamini SC of Johannesburg.

Okonkwo, who divides her practice between London and Lagos, has steadily built one of the most active African-facing arbitration books on the continent. Called to the Nigerian Bar in 2004 and admitted as a solicitor in England and Wales eight years later, she completed her doctorate in international law at the University of Geneva in 2012 under Professor Marcelo Kohen, with a thesis on the limits of investor-state arbitration in sub-Saharan Africa that is still widely cited.

"Amara is unusual in that she's equally credible to a European GC and to an African ministry of justice," said one London-based partner who has appeared before her twice. "She doesn't treat the African context as exotic — it's her home jurisdiction."

She joined the LCIA Court in 2016 and has sat on the ICC's Africa Commission since 2019. The Federal Republic of Nigeria designated her to the ICSID Panel of Arbitrators in 2018, a six-year appointment that lapsed earlier this year.

The Atlantic Gateway matter is the second large infrastructure award Okonkwo has chaired in the past 18 months. She was also presiding arbitrator in a CRCICA construction dispute concerning a rail extension in North Africa, although the outcome of that proceeding has not been made public.

In a short statement to *African Law & Business*, Okonkwo declined to discuss the award itself, citing confidentiality, but noted that "the tribunal worked hard to ensure that both the contractual record and the regulatory context received the analytical weight they deserved." She added that she expected an increasing number of disputes under the African Continental Free Trade Area framework to reach institutional arbitration over the next five years, "particularly where states have layered concession regimes on top of older investment treaties."

Okonkwo is a fellow of the Chartered Institute of Arbitrators and a founding council member of the African Arbitration Association. She is listed as a Thought Leader in *Who's Who Legal: Arbitration*.
