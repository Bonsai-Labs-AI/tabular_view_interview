## From the Chair

Dear Colleagues,

It is my privilege to write to you for the first time as Chair of the Section on Business Law. I succeeded Mr. Olumide Akpata SAN, who served the Section with distinction for two terms and to whom the entire Section is indebted. His parting address to the Section's general meeting in Abuja on 12 December 2025 will, I am told, be reproduced in the Section's annual report; I commend it to those who were unable to attend.

The first quarter of 2026 promises to be active. The Section's Business Law Conference will take place in Abuja from 22 to 26 June, and registration opened on 3 February. The conference theme — *Capital Markets, Cross-Border Investment, and the Next Decade of Nigerian Corporate Practice* — reflects our view that the regulatory and transactional environment is in genuine flux, and that practitioners must be equipped to advise across both jurisdictions and disciplines.

I draw your particular attention to the proposed revisions to the Companies and Allied Matters Act consequential regulations, which the Corporate Affairs Commission published for comment on 14 January. The Section's Companies Law Committee has drafted a substantive response, which is included as Appendix A to this bulletin. Members are encouraged to read it and to submit their own comments through the Committee or directly to the Commission before the consultation closes on 31 March.

Yours in service,

**Mrs. Hadiza Ibrahim-Bello SAN**
Chair, NBA-SBL
Lagos, 5 February 2026

## Section News

### Section Welcomes New Vice Chair

The Section is pleased to welcome **Mr. Tunde Ogunbiyi** as Vice Chair, succeeding Mrs. Ifeoma Anene who stepped down at the December general meeting. Mr. Ogunbiyi is a partner at Aluko & Oyebode and was previously Co-Chair of the Section's Securities & Capital Markets Committee. ### Annual Review Underway

The Section's annual review of business-law jurisprudence is now underway, under the editorship of Professor Olabisi Olatunji of the University of Lagos Faculty of Law. The review, which will be published in the second quarter, will cover developments in company law, securities regulation, banking, taxation, energy, and competition for the calendar year 2025. Contributions are still being accepted. The deadline for first drafts is 28 February.

### Membership Update

The Section's membership reached 4,217 members as of 31 December 2025, representing growth of 8.4 per cent over the prior year. The Section now has active branch committees in Abuja, Port Harcourt, Kano, Ibadan, and Enugu, and is in the process of establishing a sixth branch in Benin City. Members in the Edo State area are encouraged to contact the Secretariat to be added to the founding-meeting distribution list.

### Sponsorship of Junior Practitioner Programme

The Section is pleased to announce the renewal, for a further three-year term, of its corporate sponsorship arrangement with Stanbic IBTC Capital, which has supported the Junior Practitioner Programme since 2019. The programme provides bursaries, mentorship pairings, and conference subsidies to associates and senior associates in their first six years of qualified practice. Applications for the 2026 cohort opened on 1 February and close on 15 March. Eligibility criteria and the application form are available on the Section's website.

## In Memoriam

The Section records, with great sadness, the passing of the following members between 1 October 2025 and 31 January 2026:

- **Chief Akinrele Olushola SAN** (1934–2025). A senior advocate of Nigeria, founding partner of Akinrele & Co., and a member of the Section since its inception in 1992. Chief Olushola served on the Section's Companies Law Committee from 1995 to 2003 and was a long-standing contributor to the Annual Review. He is survived by his wife, four children, and twelve grandchildren.

- **Mrs. Comfort Akande-Ojo** (1968–2025). A partner at Templars and a member of the Section's Securities & Capital Markets Committee from 2017 until her appointment to the Section's Executive in 2023. Mrs. Akande-Ojo, who was 57 at the time of her death, will be remembered for her tireless mentorship of junior practitioners, particularly young women in capital markets practice. The Section has, with the agreement of her family, established the Comfort Akande-Ojo Memorial Bursary for women in their first three years of capital markets practice. Donations are welcomed and may be directed to the Secretariat.

- **Mr. Adekunle Adeoye** (1952–2026). A retired partner of Aluko & Oyebode and a member of the Section's Tax Committee for many years. Mr. Adeoye made substantial contributions to the Section's submissions on the 2007 Companies Income Tax Act amendments and the 2019 Finance Act.

- **Dr. Patrick Nwankwo** (1948–2025). Former Director-General of the Securities and Exchange Commission and an Honorary Fellow of the Section. Dr. Nwankwo is remembered for his role in the regulatory response to the 2008 capital-markets crisis and for his long teaching career at the University of Nigeria, Enugu Campus.

The Section's Executive offers its condolences to the families of all four colleagues.

## Appendix A — Companies Law Committee response to the draft CAMA consequential regulations consultation (extracts)

*[Editor's note: the full response runs to 47 pages and is available on the Section's website. The following extracts are reproduced here for the convenience of members.]*

The Committee respectfully submits that the proposed regulation 14, which would require the filing of annual confirmation statements within a 30-day window of the anniversary of incorporation rather than within the existing 42-day window, will, in the Committee's experience, generate significant administrative friction without any offsetting regulatory benefit.

The Committee further submits that regulation 22, which would extend the disclosure obligations of beneficial-ownership filings to encompass certain categories of indirect economic interest held through trust structures, requires further definitional clarity. In particular, the term "economic beneficiary" as used in draft regulation 22(3)(c) is, on the Committee's reading, capable of multiple interpretations and may give rise to inconsistent compliance practice.

The Committee endorses the broad direction of the proposed amendments to the Corporate Affairs Commission's Common Reporting Standard implementation framework but recommends that the implementation deadline be extended from 1 July 2026 to 1 January 2027 to permit affected entities to complete the necessary systems and process work.
