### Meeting 1 — September 13, 2024

**Location:** California Lawyers Association Conference Center, 400 Capitol Mall, Suite 650, Sacramento, CA, and via Zoom.

**Call to Order:** 10:03 a.m., Chair **Andrea L. Wei** presiding.

**Roll Call (Voting Members Present):**

**Absent (Excused):** Lara Echeverria (sabbatical), Dorothea Walsh

**Non-Voting / Guests:**

- Henrietta Mintz, CLA staff liaison

#### 1. Approval of Minutes

Minutes of the June 21, 2024 meeting were approved as circulated. **Motion:** Demming. **Second:** Kwan. **Vote:** 11-0-0.

#### 2. Chair's Report

Chair Wei reported that Section membership stood at **1,847** paid members as of August 31, 2024, a 3.6% increase year-over-year. The Section continues to be the third-largest of the CLA's seventeen sections by membership and the second-largest by dues revenue.

Chair Wei attended the September 4 meeting of the **CLA Board of Trustees** in San Diego and reported back that the Board has approved the Section's request to maintain its existing dues structure ($95 active / $45 inactive / $25 new admittee) through FY 2026-2027.

#### 3. Treasurer's Report

Treasurer Yoshida reported the Section's operating account at **$324,118.42** as of August 31, 2024, against an FY 2024-2025 approved budget of **$298,500** in expenditures. The reserves account stood at $612,300, well above the Section's reserves policy floor of $250,000.

Notable line items:
- **Annual Meeting & Programs:** $84,210 budgeted; $11,407 expended YTD
- **Quarterly Bulletin (print + digital):** $46,000 budgeted; $19,002 expended YTD
- **Diversity Scholarship Program:** $24,000 budgeted; $4,500 expended YTD
- **Committee Travel:** $18,000 budgeted; $2,114 expended YTD

The Treasurer's report was received without objection.

#### 4. Standing Committee Reports

##### 4.a. Mediation Standards Committee

Chair Ramaswamy reported the committee met twice over the summer and has completed a first draft of the revised **Section Recommendations on Mediator Disclosure** (Recommendation R-2024-03). The committee anticipates publishing the draft for public comment in November.

##### 4.b. Arbitration Practice Committee

Co-Chairs McNally and Castellanos reported the committee's annual survey of California arbitration practitioners closed September 1 with **412 responses**, the highest count in the survey's nine-year history. Preliminary findings include:

- 71% of respondents reported arbitrating at least one consumer dispute in the past twelve months
- 44% of respondents reported the use of a "documents-only" track in at least one matter
- Average reported time from filing to award: **10.4 months** (down from 11.2 months in the 2023 survey)

The full survey will be published in the Spring 2025 Bulletin.

##### 4.c. Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion Subcommittee

Chair Onyebuchi reported the Section's **2024 Diversity Scholarship** had been awarded to **Janelle Brooks-Atim**, a 2L at UCLA School of Law, in the amount of $4,500. The award will support Ms. Brooks-Atim's summer 2025 fellowship with the **Mosaic Mediation Center** in Los Angeles.

The subcommittee has voted to expand the 2025-2026 scholarship to two recipients at $4,000 each, subject to budget committee approval.

#### 5. Programs Committee Report

Vice Chair Demming reported the following upcoming programs:

- **October 11, 2024** — Webinar, *"Recent Developments in California Arbitration Law: 2024 in Review."* 1.5 CLE. Presenters: Hon. Patricia L. Reyes (Ret.) and Prof. Caleb Yong-Park.
- **November 8, 2024** — In-person symposium, *"Construction Defect Mediation: A Practitioner's Toolkit,"* at the Westin South Coast Plaza, Costa Mesa. 6.0 CLE. Co-sponsored with the Section's Real Property Law cousin section.
- **January 24, 2025** — Annual joint program with the State Bar's Litigation Section, *"Carve-Outs, Class Waivers, and the Aftermath of Viking River,"* at the InterContinental Mark Hopkins, San Francisco. 4.0 CLE.
- **March 7, 2025** — Webinar, *"International Commercial Arbitration for the California Practitioner: An Introduction."* 1.5 CLE. Presenter: Prof. Catalina Demont (USC Gould).

#### 6. Old Business

The committee discussed the status of the **Section comment letter** on the Judicial Council's proposed amendments to **California Rules of Court Rule 3.823** (concerning arbitrator disclosure). The committee agreed to circulate a near-final draft to members by September 27 for a vote at the next meeting.

#### 7. New Business

Member Holstrom raised the question of whether the Section should formally co-sponsor the **2025 Pacific Rim ADR Conference** in Honolulu. After discussion, the committee voted to authorize an in-kind co-sponsorship (no cash contribution) and to delegate program-content review to a working group of Holstrom, Pollack-Brent, and Zaragoza. **Motion:** Pollack-Brent. **Second:** Zaragoza. **Vote:** 10-0-1 (McNally abstaining).

#### 8. Adjournment

**Adjourned at 12:18 p.m.** Next meeting: November 15, 2024.

### Meeting 2 — November 15, 2024

YTD operating account: **$321,007.12**. YTD expenditures tracking modestly below budget. Treasurer noted higher-than-expected attendance at the October 11 webinar (387 paid registrants, against a budget of 250), producing approximately $9,200 in additional program revenue.

#### 3. Programs Update

The November 8 construction defect symposium drew 142 in-person registrants and 218 online registrants, totaling 360 across formats. Member feedback was strongly positive (average rating 4.7/5.0 across 213 returned surveys). The committee thanked the program planning team, particularly Castellanos and Pollack-Brent.

#### 4. Standing Committee Reports

##### 4.a. Mediation Standards

The draft of Recommendation R-2024-03 was distributed to members on November 1 for the 60-day public comment period. Twelve comment letters had been received as of November 14. Notable commenters included **JAMS Resolution Center** and the **Center for Public Resources / International Institute for Conflict Prevention & Resolution**.

##### 4.b. Arbitration Practice

Committee discussed the recent decision in **Veracruz Holdings, LLC v. Briarwood Capital Partners**, No. A165421 (Cal. Ct. App., 1st Dist., Oct. 17, 2024), and its implications for the enforceability of pre-dispute arbitration provisions in commercial real-estate transactions. Members were directed to review the decision in advance of a planned January 2025 webinar.

#### 5. Old Business

The Section's comment letter on proposed Rule of Court 3.823 amendments was approved by the committee, **11-0-0**, and forwarded to the CLA Board of Trustees for approval. The Board is expected to consider the letter at its December 9 meeting.

#### 6. New Business

Member Walsh proposed that the Section establish a **standing Solo and Small-Firm Practitioners Subcommittee** to address the particular concerns of ADR practitioners operating outside large-firm and judicial-officer-retired contexts. After discussion, the proposal was referred to the Bylaws Committee for review of any necessary structural amendments.

#### 7. Diversity Subcommittee Update

Onyebuchi reported the subcommittee had launched a new **mentor-matching program** pairing diverse junior attorneys interested in ADR with established Section members. Twenty-three mentor-mentee pairs were active as of November 14.

**Adjourned at 12:42 p.m.** Next meeting: February 7, 2025.

### Note on Section Composition

The Executive Committee is composed of fifteen voting members elected by the Section membership to staggered three-year terms. Officers (Chair, Vice Chair, Treasurer, Secretary) are elected by the Executive Committee from among its members, for one-year terms. The Section's standing committees are open to all Section members, and committee chairs are appointed annually by the Chair in consultation with the Vice Chair.

The Section's bylaws and policy manual are available at the CLA website. Inquiries from the membership may be directed to **Carlos R. Acuña**, CLA Sections Coordinator, at cacuña@calawyers.example.
