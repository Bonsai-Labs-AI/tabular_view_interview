# Retired N.D. Cal. Judge Tapped to Mediate Sprawling Ad-Tech Antitrust Dispute

**The Recorder** — May 2, 2024
*By Eliza Chun, Staff Reporter*

Former U.S. District Judge Michael A. Torres has been selected as the neutral mediator in a multidistrict ad-tech antitrust dispute pitting a coalition of regional newspaper publishers against a consortium of digital advertising exchanges, sources familiar with the matter confirmed this week.

The selection of Torres, 68, signals a shift toward seasoned federal judges in complex commercial mediation, where parties increasingly seek neutrals with deep antitrust pedigree. Torres, who stepped down from the bench in early 2020 after fifteen years as a district judge in the Northern District of California, was nominated to the bench in 2004 and has, since his retirement, built one of the busier ADR practices on the West Coast.

"Judge Torres is famously impatient with theatrics," said one San Francisco litigator who has appeared before him in three private arbitrations. "He reads everything. He shows up having already framed the issues. You don't waste his time."

That reputation was forged during a decade and a half on the federal bench, where Torres developed a particular expertise in antitrust class certification — including the closely-watched *In re Halverson Semiconductor* litigation, in which he certified an indirect-purchaser class in 2019. The opinion has since been cited more than 80 times by federal district courts.

Before his judicial appointment, Torres spent two decades as a litigation partner at Coleman, Pryce & Whitfield in San Francisco, where he tried more than thirty cases to verdict. He clerked for Judge Patricia M. Vallejo of the Ninth Circuit immediately after graduating from Berkeley Law in the early 1980s.

The ad-tech mediation, which involves more than $400 million in alleged damages spread across seven coordinated actions, is expected to span four to six months. A spokesperson for Torres ADR Group declined to confirm the engagement, citing the confidentiality of private dispute resolution.

Torres opened his San Francisco-based ADR shop in mid-2020, shortly after concluding his service on the bench. The firm now lists six neutrals on its roster but Torres himself handles the bulk of high-stakes commercial matters. According to publicly available reports, his calendar typically books out nine to twelve months in advance.

He is also a Fellow of the College of Commercial Arbitrators, which he joined in 2022, and serves on the AAA's Commercial Panel. Earlier this year, he was a keynote speaker at the Northern California ADR Forum, where he urged practitioners to "stop pretending arbitration is litigation with a smaller jury box."

Sources close to the ad-tech mediation said an initial joint session is expected to convene in early June at neutral facilities in downtown San Francisco. Whether the parties reach resolution before trial — a related action is set for trial in the District of Delaware in October — may turn in significant part on Torres's well-honed ability to push sophisticated litigants toward unsentimental compromise.
