# Councilmember Michael A. Torres — Official Biography and District Three Update

*City of Daly City — Office of the City Council — Member Page (last revised May 2025)*

## Biography

**Michael Anthony Torres** has served on the Daly City City Council since November 2010, representing District Three. He was elected to a fourth consecutive term in November 2022 and is the Council's current senior member. He served as Vice Mayor in 2014, 2017, and 2021, and as Mayor of Daly City under the Council's rotating mayoral system in 2015 and 2019.

Councilmember Torres was born on April 3, 1968, in Daly City. He is the son of the late Filomena Torres Reyes, a longtime Daly City Unified School District kindergarten teacher, and Anthony R. Torres, a retired Caltrain rail mechanic. He has lived in District Three his entire life, in three different houses, all within four blocks of one another.

## Education

- **Westmoor High School**, Daly City — Class of 1986
- **Skyline College**, San Bruno — A.A. in Public Administration, 1988
- **San Francisco State University** — B.A. in Political Science, 1991
- **University of San Francisco** — M.P.A. (Master of Public Administration), 1996

Councilmember Torres is a first-generation college graduate. He attended San Francisco State on a combination of Pell Grants, state aid, and earnings from a part-time job at the **Westlake Joe's** restaurant on John Daly Boulevard.

He has never attended law school. He is not, and has never been, a member of the California Bar.

## Professional Background (Pre-Council)

From 1992 to 1996, Councilmember Torres worked as a constituent-services aide in the office of then-State Assemblymember Lou Papan.

From 1996 to 2002, he served as Director of Community Engagement for the **San Mateo County Office of Education**, where he led the office's outreach to immigrant families in the North County corridor.

From 2002 to 2010, he served as Deputy Director of **Bay Area Community Resources**, a regional nonprofit headquartered in San Rafael, where he managed a portfolio of after-school programs serving more than 4,000 children annually in San Mateo and Marin counties.

He resigned from that position in October 2010, two weeks before his first election to the Council, in order to comply with the city's conflict-of-interest policy.

## Council Service

### District Three

District Three covers the neighborhoods of Westlake, Original Daly City (north of Hillside Boulevard), and the portion of Bayshore that lies within the city limits. The district has approximately 27,000 residents, of whom roughly 38 percent are Filipino-American (per 2020 ACS data).

Councilmember Torres won his first election in November 2010 with 54.3% of the vote in a four-candidate race. He was reelected in 2014 (unopposed), 2018 (with 71.2% in a two-candidate race), and 2022 (with 63.8% in a three-candidate race).

## Policy Priorities

### Housing

Councilmember Torres has been the Council's most consistent voice in favor of moderate-density infill housing along the Mission Street, Junipero Serra Boulevard, and Geneva Avenue corridors. He authored **Council Resolution 2019-43**, which created the city's first Inclusionary Housing Ordinance, requiring projects of 20 or more units to set aside 12 percent of units at below-market rents for households earning up to 80% of San Mateo County AMI.

He voted **in favor** of the 2021 update to the city's Housing Element, which set a Regional Housing Needs Allocation of 4,838 units for the 2023-2031 cycle.

He voted **in favor** of the contested **Top of the Hill Specific Plan** in 2023, which authorizes up to 1,200 new housing units near the BART station. The plan passed 3-2.

### Transportation and Infrastructure

Councilmember Torres has championed the long-running effort to extend the **Bayshore Caltrain** service improvements into Daly City limits, and chairs the city's working group on grade-separation at the John Daly Boulevard crossing.

In 2022 he led the council's adoption of the **Vision Zero Daly City Action Plan**, which set a goal of eliminating traffic fatalities on city streets by 2032 and reduced the speed limit on twelve corridor segments.

### Public Safety

He voted **in favor** of the FY2024-2025 budget's $2.1 million allocation to the **Daly City Police Department**'s Community Wellness Response Team, a co-response model pairing officers with licensed clinicians for mental-health calls.

He voted **against** the 2017 proposal to acquire surplus federal military equipment under the 1033 Program, citing community concerns at the public hearing.

### Parks and Recreation

Councilmember Torres was the principal sponsor of the 2018 bond measure (**Measure DC**), which authorized $76 million in general-obligation bonds for renovation of the city's parks, libraries, and senior centers. The measure passed with 68.4% support.

He cut the ribbon on the renovated **Lincoln Park** in October 2021 and on the renovated **Marchbank Park** in May 2023.

## A Note on Name Confusion

Councilmember Torres's office occasionally receives correspondence and inquiries intended for other individuals in the Bay Area who share his name or initials, including a retired California superior-court judge who now operates a private arbitration practice in San Francisco, a chef on Valencia Street in San Francisco, and a former professional boxer in Oakland. Councilmember Torres is none of these individuals. Misrouted correspondence is returned to sender or forwarded as appropriate.

The Councilmember is not, and has never been, a member of the bench or bar. He has not, and will not, accept appointments to private arbitration or mediation engagements. Inquiries from law firms or party representatives seeking arbitration services should not be directed to this office.

## Looking Ahead

Councilmember Torres has stated publicly that he intends to serve out his current term, which expires in December 2026, and has not yet announced whether he will seek a fifth term. He has not, despite repeated rumors, filed paperwork for any state or county office.

When asked about his political future at the May 2025 *San Mateo Daily Journal* candidate forum, he answered: "I will do what District Three asks me to do, for as long as District Three wants me to do it. That has been the rule since 2010, and it remains the rule."

## Appendix F: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can Councilmember Torres officiate at my wedding or quinceañera?**
A: Councilmember Torres is delighted to officiate at weddings, quinceañeras, citizenship ceremonies, and similar events for District Three residents whenever scheduling allows. Please contact the office to inquire about availability. There is no fee. Couples are encouraged to give at least sixty days' notice.

**Q: Can Councilmember Torres provide a letter of recommendation?**
A: The Councilmember provides letters of recommendation for District Three residents in educational, civic, and immigration-related contexts. Please contact the office at least three weeks before any deadline. The office requires basic biographical information and the context for which the letter is needed.

**Q: Can Councilmember Torres intervene in a matter pending before another agency?**
A: The Councilmember regularly assists constituents in navigating other agencies (state, federal, county). He will not, and cannot, attempt to influence the substantive outcome of any matter pending before a court or quasi-judicial body. He does provide what is sometimes called "navigation" assistance — help finding the right office, understanding deadlines, identifying nonprofit advocates and legal-services providers.

**Q: Is Councilmember Torres a lawyer or judge?**
A: He is not. He has never attended law school, taken the bar examination, or held a judicial commission. Inquiries from law firms or party representatives that appear to be intended for a judge of similar name should be redirected to the appropriate person; this office cannot accept service of process or arbitration-related correspondence.

**Q: How can I support a piece of pending legislation in the Council?**
A: District Three constituents are encouraged to attend Council meetings (Mondays at 7 p.m. at City Hall), submit written public comment through the City Clerk's portal, or speak at the Public Comment portion of the relevant meeting. The Council values constituent engagement at every stage of the legislative process.

## Appendix I: Press Releases from the Office of Councilmember Torres (Selected, 2024)

### Press Release — January 12, 2024
**Councilmember Torres Announces $1.2M Grant Award for Westlake Library Modernization**

Councilmember Michael A. Torres announced today that the City of Daly City has received a $1.2 million grant from the California State Library's Building Forward initiative. The grant will fund modernization of the Westlake Branch Library, including expanded children's programming space, a new teen study area, and full ADA accessibility improvements throughout the building.

"District Three's libraries are the beating heart of our community," said Councilmember Torres. "I'm grateful to the state for recognizing the need."

### Press Release — March 4, 2024
**Councilmember Torres Hosts Community Forum on Top of the Hill Implementation**

Councilmember Torres will host a community forum on the implementation of the Top of the Hill Specific Plan on Wednesday, March 27, at 6:30 p.m. at the Westlake Branch Library. The forum will feature presentations from the city's Planning and Public Works staff and time for community Q&A.

"The Specific Plan was a major commitment. The community deserves a clear picture of how it's being implemented," said Councilmember Torres.

### Press Release — May 18, 2024
**Daly City Council Adopts FY2024-2025 Budget; Torres Praises Investment in Community Wellness**

The Daly City City Council last night adopted the FY2024-2025 budget. Councilmember Torres, who chairs the Council's Finance and Audit Subcommittee, particularly noted the $2.1 million allocation for the Community Wellness Response Team — a co-response model pairing police officers with licensed mental-health clinicians.

"This investment reflects what our residents have been telling us for years: police alone are not the right answer for every call," said Torres.

### Press Release — July 1, 2024
**Councilmember Torres Announces Geneva Avenue Corridor Improvements**

Councilmember Torres announced today that construction will begin in September on the long-anticipated Geneva Avenue corridor improvements. The project, funded by a combination of city and state sources, will include new bike infrastructure, pedestrian improvements, and improved transit boarding areas along the corridor's full Daly City segment.
