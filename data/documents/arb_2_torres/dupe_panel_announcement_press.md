## Northern California ADR Forum Confirms Featured Panel for 2024 Annual Meeting
### *"After the Bench: Former Federal Judges as Commercial Neutrals — Practice, Pitfalls, and Perception"*
### Hon. Michael Torres (Ret.) of Torres ADR Group LLC to Moderate Friday Morning Plenary Session at Palace Hotel

**SAN FRANCISCO, August 13, 2024** — The **Northern California ADR Forum** (NCADRF) today confirmed the lineup for one of the featured plenary panels of its 2024 Annual Meeting, to be held Friday, September 27, 2024, at the historic **Palace Hotel** in downtown San Francisco. The panel — *"After the Bench: Former Federal Judges as Commercial Neutrals — Practice, Pitfalls, and Perception"* — will convene from 10:15 a.m. to 11:45 a.m. PDT in the Palace Hotel's **Ralston Ballroom**.

The plenary will be moderated by **Hon. Michael Torres (Ret.)**, the founder of San Francisco-based **Torres ADR Group LLC** and a former United States District Judge for the Northern District of California. Joining Judge Torres on the panel are:

- **Hon. Linda M. Chao (Ret.)** — Neutral, JAMS;
- **Prof. Daniel Verma** — UC Hastings College of the Law; and
- **Marcia O'Donnell, Esq.** — General Counsel, Trellis Software, Inc.

### About the Moderator

Judge Torres served as a United States District Judge for the Northern District of California from 2004 until his retirement from the federal bench. He now maintains a full-time commercial arbitration and mediation practice in San Francisco. He is a **Fellow of the College of Commercial Arbitrators**, a member of the **American Arbitration Association's Commercial Panel**, and is admitted to practice in California, the District of Columbia, and New York.

Since opening Torres ADR Group LLC, Judge Torres has served as arbitrator, mediator, or special master in matters involving commercial contracts, technology licensing, financial services, and post-judgment disputes. He has been a regular speaker at programs of the American Bar Association's Dispute Resolution Section, the AAA-ICDR's Master Class series, and the Northern California ADR Forum.

### About the Panel

The plenary will explore the career trajectory — increasingly common over the past two decades — from federal judicial service into private commercial dispute resolution. Panelists will address how former judges adapt their decision-making style to the arbitral context, manage party expectations, and navigate the market dynamics of an ADR market crowded with retired-judge neutrals.

Topics to be addressed include:

- The transition from "adjudicate" to "neutral": shifts in posture, demeanor, and disclosure practice;
- Best practices for disclosure under California Code of Civil Procedure §§ 1281.9 and 1281.85, the AAA Code of Ethics, and the JAMS Arbitrators Ethics Guidelines;
- The market for retired-judge neutrals and the question of differentiation in a maturing market;
- The interaction between former-judge habits of bench management and the procedural flexibility of arbitration;
- The view from the in-house counsel and academic perspectives on the strengths and limitations of the former-judge model.

### About the Northern California ADR Forum

The Northern California ADR Forum is a membership organization of approximately 940 California ADR professionals — arbitrators, mediators, special masters, advocates, and academics — based primarily in the Bay Area, Sacramento, and the Central Coast. The Forum holds an annual meeting in San Francisco each fall, publishes a quarterly bulletin (the *Forum Review*), and operates programming and mentorship initiatives focused on emerging neutrals and on diversity in the field.

The Forum was founded in 1996 as the **Bay Area Mediator and Arbitrator Society** and adopted its current name in 2008. Its current president is **Hannah Reilly-Petrov**, General Counsel of Solstice Devices, Inc.

## Supplementary Attachment D — Forum History and Mission

The Northern California ADR Forum was founded in 1996 as the **Bay Area Mediator and Arbitrator Society** by a small group of practitioners — including the late **John Tao**, **Patricia Anders-Falk**, and **Robert Mendelson-Owen** — who sought to create a professional community for the then-emerging cohort of full-time neutrals in the Bay Area.

The Society incorporated as a California nonprofit in 1997 and adopted its current name, the **Northern California ADR Forum**, in 2008 in recognition of its expanded geographic and professional scope.

The Forum's mission, as stated in its current bylaws, is:

> "To advance the practice of alternative dispute resolution in Northern California through programming, scholarship, mentorship, and advocacy; to promote diversity, equity, and inclusion in the field; and to support the continued professional development of ADR practitioners across all stages of career."

The Forum currently has approximately 940 active members. Membership is open to all California-based ADR practitioners, including arbitrators, mediators, special masters, advocates, and academics. Annual dues are $185 for full members, $95 for academic members, and $45 for student members.

The Forum's principal annual events are:

- **The Annual Meeting** (late September, San Francisco) — the Forum's flagship two-day event, featuring plenary panels, breakouts, awards, and networking.
- **The Winter Workshop** (January, location varies) — a one-day intensive on a focused topic.
- **The Spring Symposium** (April, often paired with an academic institution) — a half-day scholarly program.
- **The Summer Reception** (June, San Francisco) — an informal members-only social event.

The Forum also publishes a quarterly bulletin, the *Forum Review*, with original scholarship, practice notes, and event coverage.
