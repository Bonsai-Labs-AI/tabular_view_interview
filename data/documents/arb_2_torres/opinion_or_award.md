## I. Procedural Background

This matter arises out of a Master Services Agreement dated March 11, 2019 (the "MSA"), under which Claimant Meridian Analytics, Inc. ("Meridian") agreed to provide custom data-engineering and predictive-modeling services to Respondent Karsten Biosciences Corporation ("Karsten") in connection with Karsten's Phase II clinical trial pipeline. Section 18.4 of the MSA contains a broad arbitration clause designating the American Arbitration Association and selecting California substantive law.

Meridian filed its Demand for Arbitration on October 7, 2022, asserting claims for breach of contract, breach of the implied covenant of good faith and fair dealing, and quantum meruit, and seeking damages in the amount of $4.62 million plus prejudgment interest. Karsten filed an Answer and Counterclaim on November 18, 2022, asserting breach of warranty and seeking offset of approximately $1.8M.

The Arbitrator was appointed by AAA on December 9, 2022, following the parties' inability to agree on a neutral from the AAA Commercial Roster strike list. A preliminary hearing was conducted on January 11, 2023. A merits hearing was held over five days, from June 5 through June 9, 2023, at the AAA hearing facility in San Francisco. The parties submitted post-hearing briefs on July 14, 2023.

## II. Findings of Fact

After considering the testimony of nine fact witnesses and two expert witnesses, and reviewing the documentary record (Exhibits C-1 through C-214 and R-1 through R-156), the Arbitrator finds as follows:

1. The MSA's Statement of Work No. 3 ("SOW-3"), executed July 22, 2020, required Meridian to deliver a validated predictive model with a minimum area-under-curve ("AUC") of 0.82 on Karsten's held-out validation dataset.
2. Meridian delivered an initial model on January 4, 2021, achieving an AUC of 0.79. After two iteration cycles, the model achieved AUC of 0.84 on November 9, 2021, satisfying the SOW-3 acceptance criterion.
3. Karsten's refusal to issue the acceptance certificate required under Section 7.2 of the MSA was not made in good faith. *See Carma Developers (Cal.), Inc. v. Marathon Dev. Cal., Inc.*, 2 Cal. 4th 342, 372 (1992).

## III. Conclusions of Law

Under California law, a party may not invoke a discretionary acceptance provision in a manner that frustrates the other party's reasonable expectations under the contract. *Guz v. Bechtel Nat'l, Inc.*, 24 Cal. 4th 317, 349–50 (2000); *Locke v. Warner Bros., Inc.*, 57 Cal. App. 4th 354, 363 (1997). The Arbitrator concludes that Karsten breached Sections 7.2 and 12.1 of the MSA.

## IV. Award

For the foregoing reasons, the Arbitrator AWARDS Claimant the principal sum of **$3,847,206.00**, together with prejudgment interest at the California statutory rate of 10% per annum from December 1, 2021 through the date of this Award. Karsten's counterclaims are DENIED in their entirety.

Administrative fees and expenses of the AAA totaling $74,300 and the compensation of the Arbitrator totaling $182,750 shall be borne 75% by Respondent and 25% by Claimant.

This Award resolves all claims and counterclaims submitted to this arbitration.
