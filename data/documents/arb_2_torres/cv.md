## Judicial Service

**United States District Court, Northern District of California**
*United States District Judge* — January 2005 to March 2020

Nominated by President George W. Bush; confirmed by the U.S. Senate by voice vote on December 16, 2004. Presided over a docket of approximately 350 active civil and criminal matters at any given time, with a particular concentration in complex commercial litigation, antitrust class actions, and Title VII employment matters. Served as a member of the Court's Civil Rules Advisory Committee from 2011 to 2017.

## ADR Experience

Since retiring from the federal bench in 2020, Judge Torres has served as a full-time neutral, accepting appointments through Torres ADR Group as well as through JAMS and the American Arbitration Association. He has presided over more than 180 arbitrations and mediations, with concentrations in:

- Executive employment disputes (wrongful termination, equity claw-backs, restrictive covenants)
- Commercial contract disputes involving technology licensing and SaaS agreements
- Post-merger indemnification and earn-out disputes
- Antitrust and unfair-competition matters

## Notable Bench Decisions

- *In re Halverson Semiconductor Antitrust Litig.*, 412 F. Supp. 3d 884 (N.D. Cal. 2019) — class certification of indirect-purchaser claims
- *Reyes v. Aldritch Logistics, Inc.*, 287 F. Supp. 3d 1101 (N.D. Cal. 2018) — summary judgment in FLSA collective action
- *Sentinel Holdings LLC v. Bracewell Capital Partners*, 2017 WL 4488213 (N.D. Cal. Oct. 9, 2017) — denial of motion to compel arbitration on unconscionability grounds
