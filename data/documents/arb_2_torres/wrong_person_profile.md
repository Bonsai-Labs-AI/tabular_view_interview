# From the Ring to the Family Restaurant: A Bay Area Original

**East Bay Express — Local Profiles** | August 22, 2017

For three decades, Michael Torres has been a fixture at his family's restaurant in Oakland's Fruitvale district, where the carnitas tacos and his own boxing memorabilia draw a steady crowd most weekday lunchtimes.

Torres, 64, fought professionally between 1976 and 1986, finishing his career with a 23-7 record as a light middleweight. He fought twice at the Cow Palace in Daly City and once on the undercard of a Marvin Hagler tune-up bout in Worcester, Massachusetts, in 1981. After retiring from the ring he took over the restaurant his father opened in 1969, "Torres y Hijos," and has run it ever since with his wife Carmen and their two daughters.

"People come in and ask about the gloves on the wall," Torres said in a recent interview, gesturing to a pair of red Everlast gloves displayed near the cash register. "That's how I know they're either old fight fans or they read the East Bay Express. Either way, I'll tell them the story."

The restaurant is closed on Sundays.
