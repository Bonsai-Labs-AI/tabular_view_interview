# Review: Tanaka, Berkeley — Three Stars

**San Francisco Chronicle — Restaurant Review** | April 18, 2022

When chef Yuki Tanaka left her sous-chef post at State Bird Provisions in late 2019 to open a small kappo-style room in a converted Berkeley bungalow, she did so with sufficient quiet conviction that the restaurant world, locked down through most of 2020 and 2021 anyway, more or less missed it. Two years on, with the dining room finally full at every service, Tanaka is a destination — and one of the most assured Japanese kitchens in the Bay Area.

Tanaka, 38, grew up in Sapporo and trained at Tsuji Culinary Institute in Osaka before moving to the United States in 2011. Her room seats fourteen, the menu changes weekly, and the dégustation runs to twelve courses for $235. The chef does almost all of the cooking herself, with a single sous-chef and one server in the dining room.

The standout course on a recent visit was an aged-tai sashimi over warmed soy and yuzu kosho, served with a small bowl of clear dashi. The sashimi had the slight stickiness of a fish that has rested under a kelp wrap for two days, and the dashi — single-source kombu, water, light shoyu — was clean enough to seem austere.

**Reservations** open thirty days in advance via Tock. Closed Sunday and Monday.
