## I. Introduction and Procedural History

1. This Partial Final Award is rendered in an arbitration commenced by Sakura Microelectronics Corporation ("Sakura" or "Claimant"), a kabushiki kaisha organized under the laws of Japan with its principal place of business in Yokohama, against Pacific Logic Devices, Inc. ("PLD" or "Respondent"), a Delaware corporation with its principal place of business in San Jose, California.

2. The dispute arises out of the Patent Cross-License and Foundry Supply Agreement dated 1 April 2018 (the "PCLA"), pursuant to which Sakura granted PLD a non-exclusive license to a portfolio of 287 patents in the field of low-power CMOS logic and embedded SRAM (the "Licensed Patents"), in exchange for running royalties calculated on Net Sales of Licensed Products and a most-favored-licensee ("MFL") undertaking set forth at Article 7.3.

3. The Request for Arbitration was filed on 11 May 2022. Following the parties' agreement, the undersigned was confirmed as Sole Arbitrator on 8 July 2022. A procedural hearing was held by videoconference on 22 July 2022, at which Procedural Order No. 1 was issued. Evidentiary hearings were conducted in Tokyo from 9 to 13 January 2023, with simultaneous interpretation into Japanese and English.

## II. The Dispute in Summary

4. Claimant contends that Respondent has materially underreported Net Sales for fiscal years 2019 through 2021 by improperly excluding revenue from "system-on-chip" products in which the Licensed Patents are embodied, on the theory that such SoCs fall outside the definition of "Licensed Products" in Section 1.14 of the PCLA. Claimant seeks (i) a declaration that the SoC products are Licensed Products; (ii) payment of unpaid royalties in the amount of U.S.$71,400,000, plus pre-award interest; and (iii) an order directing a forensic audit pursuant to Article 9.

5. Respondent denies the foregoing and counterclaims that Claimant breached the MFL clause by extending a 1.25% royalty rate to a third-party licensee (a Korean memory manufacturer) in December 2020, while continuing to invoice Respondent at the contractual 2.10% rate.

## III. Findings on Contract Interpretation

6. The Tribunal has carefully considered the textual, contextual, and negotiating-history evidence under California Civil Code §§ 1636–1647. The definition of "Licensed Products" at Section 1.14 is, on its face, broad: it captures "any integrated circuit, the manufacture, use, sale, or import of which would, but for the licenses granted hereunder, infringe one or more Valid Claims of the Licensed Patents." The Tribunal does not accept Respondent's argument that the parties' course of dealing in 2016–2017, under a predecessor agreement, narrows that definition. As the California Supreme Court has observed, course of dealing cannot vary unambiguous express terms. *See Pacific Gas & Elec. Co. v. G.W. Thomas Drayage & Rigging Co.*, 69 Cal. 2d 33 (1968).

7. Accordingly, the Tribunal finds that the SoC products identified in Claimant's Exhibit C-114 are Licensed Products within the meaning of Section 1.14.

## IV. Findings on the MFL Counterclaim

8. The MFL clause is engaged. However, the Tribunal finds that the rate differential is partially justified by the differing scope of the third-party grant (which excludes foundry rights). The royalty owed by Respondent shall be adjusted prospectively to 1.65%, effective from the date of the third-party license.
