# Tanaka Tapped to Chair Tribunal in $900M U.S.–Japan Chip Patent Spat

*Managing IP — 22 November 2023*
*By Hannah Ueno, Tokyo Correspondent*

Veteran arbitrator Dr. Yuki Tanaka has been confirmed as president of a three-member ICDR tribunal in what sources describe as one of the largest semiconductor patent disputes of the year, pitting a U.S.-listed memory company against a Japanese equipment manufacturer over a long-running cross-license. The amount in controversy is understood to exceed U.S.$900 million.

Tanaka, who splits her practice between Palo Alto and Tokyo, was nominated by the two party-appointed arbitrators after the parties failed to agree on a chair within the contractual window. People familiar with the matter say her dual U.S.–Japan qualification and her facility with technical evidence in both languages were determinative. The arbitration is seated in Singapore and will apply New York law.

"Yuki is one of the very few arbitrators who can run a wafer-fab patent case in two languages without losing a beat," said one senior counsel at a Magic Circle firm in Tokyo, who has appeared before her twice. "She runs a tight procedural ship and she actually reads the prior art."

Tanaka, 48, took her doctorate at Stanford Law School in 2010, where her dissertation work on FRAND obligations in cross-border licensing has since been cited by Japanese and Korean courts. Before turning full-time to arbitration in 2015, she practiced in the Tokyo office of a U.S. firm, where she advised on the licensing programs of several major Japanese electronics conglomerates. She holds bar admissions in California and as a Japanese bengoshi, and is registered as a gaikokuho-jimu-bengoshi in Tokyo.

She sits on the panels of the JCAA, AAA-ICDR, SIAC, and most recently — as of last spring — the Hong Kong International Arbitration Centre, where she was added to the IP-specialist roster. She is also an adjunct professor at Keio University, where she co-teaches a popular seminar on semiconductor licensing.

Industry observers note that the new appointment is consistent with a recent trend of high-value Asia-seated patent arbitrations being chaired by arbitrators with deep semiconductor sector knowledge. Tanaka's recent docket has reportedly included a JCAA-administered cross-licensing dispute involving a Japanese IDM and a U.S. fabless designer (publicly believed to be in the ¥18 billion range), and a FRAND rate-setting dispute over a 4G/5G SEP portfolio.

In a brief comment to *Managing IP*, Tanaka declined to discuss the new appointment but observed: "There is more cross-border IP arbitration in this corridor than there was five years ago, and the cases are more technically complex. That is good for the parties — courts are not always set up for this."

A first procedural conference in the new ICDR matter is scheduled for early December. A merits hearing is not expected before late 2024.
