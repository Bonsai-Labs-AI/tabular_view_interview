# Eleanor Vance, Folk Singer and Songwriter, Dies at 78

**The Boston Globe — Obituaries** | February 11, 2019

Eleanor Vance, a Cambridge-born folk singer whose songs about labor organizing in the New England mill towns earned her a devoted following on the East Coast coffeehouse circuit in the late 1960s and 1970s, died at her home in Truro, Massachusetts, on Saturday. She was 78.

Born in 1940 to a Welsh father who worked in the Watertown arsenal and an Irish mother who taught piano, Ms. Vance was raised in a household where music was a daily presence. She picked up the guitar at twelve, dropped out of Boston University to play full-time at eighteen, and recorded three studio albums for Folkways between 1968 and 1974. Her best-known song, "Lawrence Mill Lights," was covered by Joan Baez on the 1975 anthology *Bread and Roses*.

In later years she taught songwriting at the Harvard Extension School and ran an annual songwriting workshop on Cape Cod. She is survived by her partner of forty years, the photographer Margaret Lillis, and a brother, Conor Vance, of Galway.

A memorial service will be held at the Provincetown Unitarian Universalist Meeting House on March 2.
