# London Court of International Arbitration

## Quarterly Caseload and Institutional Report

### Q4 2023 — Released 14 February 2024

---

### Foreword by the Registrar

This Quarterly Caseload and Institutional Report, the twenty-seventh in the series, provides registered users and the broader arbitration community with a current picture of the LCIA's caseload, institutional activity, and governance developments through 31 December 2023. As in prior quarters, the data presented here represent cases administered by the LCIA Secretariat under the LCIA Rules; LCIA-administered cases proceeding under the UNCITRAL Rules are tabulated separately and identified as such. Cases administered jointly with other institutions — most notably the four cases administered jointly with the DIFC-LCIA legacy caseload during the orderly wind-down period — are excluded from the principal tables and discussed separately at Section VI.

The Court is pleased to report a fourth-quarter case-referral volume of 117 new requests for arbitration, the highest single-quarter total since Q1 2021, bringing the calendar-year 2023 total to 423 referrals. This represents a 6.8 percent increase over calendar year 2022 (396 referrals) and a 12.3 percent increase over the rolling five-year average. The Q4 figure is consistent with the broader pattern of increased referrals in the energy, banking and financial-services, and shipping sectors observed throughout the year.

We are also pleased to note the conclusion of the LCIA's relocation to its new premises at 110 Cannon Street and the formal commencement of operations of the LCIA Casework Technology Modernisation Programme on 1 December 2023.

### II. Sectoral Composition of Referrals

The Q4 2023 referrals show a continuation of the sectoral pattern observed throughout 2023, with banking and financial services again leading at 27.4 percent of new referrals, followed by energy and resources (19.7 percent), shipping and transport (12.0 percent), construction and infrastructure (10.3 percent), and telecommunications and technology (8.5 percent). The remaining 22.1 percent is distributed across professional services, consumer goods, life sciences, hospitality, and miscellaneous categories.

The growth in energy-and-resources referrals — up 38.1 percent year-over-year — is attributable principally to a sharp increase in upstream-and-midstream gas-infrastructure disputes (eleven new referrals in Q4 alone, against an average of four per quarter in 2021–2022) and to continued referrals arising from the long-term-supply-agreement disputes that have characterised the post-2022 energy market.

Banking-and-financial-services referrals continue to be dominated by ISDA Master Agreement, loan-documentation, and structured-product disputes, with an emerging cluster of referrals concerning carbon-credit purchase agreements and voluntary-market disputes (seven new referrals in Q4, against two in all of 2022).

The respondent-nationality distribution is broadly similar to the claimant distribution, with the notable exception that Middle East / North Africa respondents account for a substantially higher share of respondent appearances (17.9 percent) than of claimant appearances. This continues a pattern observed since approximately 2019.

### IV. Tribunal Composition and Diversity Statistics

#### A. Total Arbitrator Appointments

A total of 327 arbitrator appointments were confirmed by the LCIA Court in calendar 2023. Of these:

- 198 (60.6%) were party-nominated
- 92 (28.1%) were nominated by the Court directly
- 37 (11.3%) were nominated by co-arbitrators

The mean number of arbitrators per tribunal stood at 2.31, broadly consistent with the 2022 figure of 2.27.

#### B. Gender Representation

Female arbitrators constituted 41.2 percent of LCIA-Court appointments in 2023, a 2.1 percentage-point increase over 2022 (39.1 percent). Female arbitrators constituted only 27.4 percent of party nominations, however, indicating that the Court's institutional commitment to gender diversity continues to outpace party practice in this respect.

#### C. Geographic Diversity

Arbitrators of African nationality accounted for 6.4 percent of all 2023 appointments (an increase from 4.8 percent in 2022), continuing a slow but steady upward trend. Arbitrators of Latin American nationality accounted for 3.1 percent of all 2023 appointments, a figure the Court considers low relative to claimant-respondent geography and to which it expects to give increased attention in 2024.

### V. Timeline and Efficiency Metrics

The mean time from request for arbitration to constitution of the tribunal in Q4 2023 was 84 days, a slight increase over Q3 2023 (78 days) but well within historical norms. The mean time from constitution of the tribunal to final award was 14.3 months for awards rendered in Q4 2023, a figure substantially affected by the inclusion of three multi-party energy matters with extended evidentiary phases.

The Court continues to monitor compliance with Article 15.10 of the LCIA Rules (the six-month indicative target for issuance of awards after closure of proceedings). In Q4 2023, 71.4 percent of awards were rendered within the indicative target, against 68.9 percent in 2022 and 64.1 percent in 2021.

### VI. DIFC-LCIA Legacy Caseload Wind-Down

The orderly wind-down of the LCIA's administration of the DIFC-LCIA legacy caseload continued through the fourth quarter. As of 31 December 2023, four legacy matters remained open, against eleven as of the end of 2022. The Secretariat anticipates that the legacy caseload will be substantially closed by the end of Q2 2024.

### VII. Governance Developments

#### A. Court Appointments

The LCIA Board of Directors confirmed the appointment of two new Vice-Presidents of the Court at its November 2023 meeting:

- **Sir Harold Pemberton-Quincy KC**, of Brick Court Chambers, London, with effect from 1 January 2024.
- **Maître Inès Diallo-Hervé**, of Bredin Prat (Paris), with effect from 1 January 2024.

These appointments bring the total Court membership to 36, with female representation at 47.2 percent.

#### B. Departures

The Court records with thanks the conclusion of the terms of:

- **Justice Margarethe Lindgren-Sten** (Stockholm), Vice-President, 2018–2023
- **Dr Anil Chatterjee SC** (Singapore), Court Member, 2019–2023

#### C. Secretariat

The Court is pleased to welcome **Ms Olubunmi Ade-Ogundele** as Deputy Registrar with effect from 8 January 2024. Ms Ade-Ogundele joins the LCIA from the Permanent Court of Arbitration, where she served as Senior Legal Counsel from 2017 to 2023.

### VIII. Premises and Technology

The LCIA's relocation from its former premises at 70 Fleet Street to new premises at 110 Cannon Street was completed on 28 October 2023. The new premises offer six hearing rooms (three with simultaneous-interpretation capacity), a dedicated tribunal-deliberation suite, and improved hybrid-hearing infrastructure throughout. The Court records its thanks to the Property Committee and to facilities manager Mr Brendan O'Kelly for the smooth execution of the move.

The LCIA Casework Technology Modernisation Programme — a three-year programme to replace the Secretariat's existing case-management system with a purpose-built platform supporting fully electronic filing, document management, and party self-service — commenced operations on 1 December 2023. Phase 1 (electronic filing and document management) is now live for all new cases; Phase 2 (party self-service portal) is expected to go live in Q3 2024.

### IX. Outreach and Education

The LCIA's outreach programme delivered 27 events in calendar 2023, including:

- The annual LCIA European Users' Council symposium (Brussels, March 2023)
- A joint conference with HKIAC on cross-border energy disputes (Hong Kong, June 2023)
- The LCIA Young International Arbitration Group (YIAG) Tylney Hall workshop (Hampshire, September 2023)
- A series of regional users' council meetings in Lagos, Mumbai, and São Paulo

The LCIA's mentoring scheme for early-career practitioners enrolled 78 mentees in its 2023 cohort, against 64 in 2022. The Court records its thanks to the 41 senior practitioners who served as mentors during the year.

### X. Financial Position

The LCIA closed its financial year on 30 September 2023 in a sound financial position, with operating revenues of £18.4 million (against £16.9 million in FY 2022) and operating expenses of £14.1 million (against £13.2 million). The surplus has been allocated principally to the Technology Modernisation Programme reserve. The audited accounts will be published with the LCIA's Annual Report in May 2024.

### XI. Footnotes and Methodology

1. All amounts in dispute are converted to U.S. dollars at the Bank of England spot rate as of the date of request for arbitration.
2. The "pending caseload" figure includes all cases for which a request for arbitration has been registered but for which no final award has been rendered and no settlement, withdrawal, or termination has been recorded.
3. Sectoral classification follows the LCIA's internal classification scheme, which is broadly aligned with but does not precisely correspond to the GICS Level 1 classification.
4. Arbitrator nationality is recorded by reference to the nationality of the passport held at the time of appointment; arbitrators holding multiple nationalities are recorded by reference to their primary professional residence.
5. "Female arbitrator" reflects self-identification at the time of LCIA Court confirmation.

### Appendix E — Note on Methodology

The data presented in this report are drawn from the LCIA Secretariat's internal case-management records as of 31 December 2023. Definitions of "referral," "registered case," and "constituted tribunal" follow the conventions set out in the Annex to the LCIA's published statistical methodology (revised May 2022 and available on request).

Where this report presents averages or other summary statistics, the underlying calculation is performed on the population of cases registered during the relevant calendar period. Where a case spans multiple reporting periods, the case is allocated to a reporting period by reference to the date on which the relevant transactional event (referral, constitution, award) was recorded in the case-management system.
