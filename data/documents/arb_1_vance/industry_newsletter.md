# Global Arbitration Review — Weekly Brief

**Issue 1847** | May 9, 2024

## Caseloads continue to climb at major institutions

The ICC, LCIA, and SIAC all reported double-digit growth in new arbitration filings during the first quarter of 2024, according to their quarterly statistics releases this week. ICC filings rose 14% year-over-year, with energy disputes and post-acquisition indemnification claims continuing to make up the largest share of new matters.

## Tribunal secretaries: the unresolved debate

A working group convened by the Swiss Arbitration Association is preparing a draft guideline on the proper role of tribunal secretaries in international commercial arbitration. The draft, expected to be circulated for consultation in late summer, will address fee disclosure, scope of permissible drafting assistance, and the boundary between secretarial work and tribunal deliberation.

## Around the chambers

Three Crowns has opened a small office in Madrid focused on Latin American investment treaty work. Freshfields has elevated four new arbitration partners across its London and Paris offices. The Vienna International Arbitral Centre has appointed a new Deputy Secretary General.
