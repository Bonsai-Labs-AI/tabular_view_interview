## Vance Tapped To Chair $2B Cross-Border Energy Arbitration

**By Marcus Hellinger** | March 4, 2024, 6:42 PM EST

Prof. Eleanor Vance, the Columbia Law professor-turned-arbitrator known for her work on high-stakes commercial disputes, has been appointed chair of a three-member ICC tribunal hearing claims valued at more than $2 billion between a Brazilian state-owned energy company and a consortium of European pipeline operators, two sources with knowledge of the matter told Law360 on Monday.

The dispute, seated in Paris and governed by Swiss substantive law, arises from a 2014 joint operating agreement covering offshore gas infrastructure. The claimants allege that capacity-allocation decisions made between 2020 and 2022 breached preferential-access provisions in the JOA. Counsel for the parties — understood to include teams from Three Crowns and Freshfields — declined to comment, citing confidentiality.

Vance, 59, is among a small group of U.S. academics who have built substantial full-time arbitration practices since leaving the classroom. She joined the Columbia Law School faculty in 2001 after five years at Georgetown, and was appointed the Charles Evans Hughes Professor of Law in 2009. She took emerita status in 2022 to focus on her chambers practice, which she runs from offices on Park Avenue in Midtown Manhattan.

"Eleanor brings a rare combination of doctrinal depth and procedural discipline," said Robert Kaminski of NYU Law, her longtime co-author. "She is willing to write long, reasoned awards, which parties increasingly value when they know an annulment challenge is likely."

A Rhodes Scholar who took her first law degree at Oxford in 1988 before earning her J.D. from Yale in 1991, Vance clerked for Judge Patricia Wald on the D.C. Circuit before joining the academy. Her treatise with Kaminski, *The Law and Practice of Commercial Arbitration*, is in its third edition and is widely assigned in U.S. and U.K. LL.M. programs.

Vance has chaired or sat as sole arbitrator in more than 80 commercial matters over the past decade. Recent publicly reported appointments include a $1.4 billion post-acquisition indemnification dispute administered by the ICC in 2020 — in which she issued a partial final award on liability against the seller-respondents — and an LCIA reinsurance matter seated in London.

She is also active in institutional governance. Vance served on the ICC Commission on Arbitration and ADR from 2015 to 2021 and currently sits on the board of the New York International Arbitration Center.

Sources close to the new ICC proceeding said the tribunal is expected to hold a procedural conference later this spring, with an evidentiary hearing likely to take place in early 2025. A final award would not be expected before late 2025 at the earliest.
