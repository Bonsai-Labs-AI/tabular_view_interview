## Columbia Law School Announces Four Major Faculty Appointments and Two Endowed Chair Designations

### Incoming Faculty Strengthen the Law School's Programs in Antitrust, Constitutional Law, International Tax, and Critical Race Theory

**NEW YORK** — Dean Adelaide Vasquez-Brindisi announced today four new permanent faculty appointments at Columbia Law School, effective with the 2023–2024 academic year, together with the designation of two existing faculty members to named professorial chairs. The new appointments — which follow more than eighteen months of extended faculty searches — represent the largest single cohort of tenured and tenure-track hires at the Law School since the 2014–2015 academic year, and reflect what the Dean described in remarks to the faculty earlier this week as "a deliberate, generational investment in the intellectual depth of the school across multiple subject areas of strategic importance."

The four new appointments are:

- **Professor Bartolomeo Calligari-Ng** (Antitrust and Industrial Organization), joining as the Henry W. and Joan T. Voorhees Professor of Law from Berkeley Law;
- **Professor Saoirse Murchadh-Renwick** (Constitutional Law), joining as Professor of Law from the University of Chicago Law School;
- **Professor Hiroshi Akinwumi-Delacroix** (International Tax), joining as Associate Professor of Law from NYU School of Law; and
- **Professor Ndidi Rosalind Babatunde-Cariou** (Critical Race Theory and Civil Procedure), joining as the inaugural Patricia Williams Professor of Law from Georgetown Law.

In addition, two existing members of the Columbia Law faculty have been designated to named chairs:

- **Professor Caleb Wenzhou Halberstam** has been designated the Michael I. Sovern Professor of Law, succeeding the late Professor Lance Liebman.
- **Professor Marisela Quiñones-Eastman** has been designated the Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Professor of Law.

### About the New Appointments

#### Professor Bartolomeo Calligari-Ng — Henry W. and Joan T. Voorhees Professor of Law

Professor Calligari-Ng, who joins Columbia Law School from Berkeley Law, is one of the country's most cited antitrust scholars under the age of fifty. His scholarship draws on industrial-organization economics, regulatory theory, and competition-law doctrine, and is concerned principally with the analysis of vertical integration in digital platforms and with the institutional design of antitrust enforcement.

His major works include *Vertical Lines: Doctrine and Discipline in Modern Antitrust* (Harvard University Press, 2019); *The Platform Conglomerate* (Yale Law Journal, vol. 130, no. 4, 2021); and the casebook *Antitrust Law and Economics* (with Drosselmeier and Yashin, West Academic, 5th ed. 2022).

Professor Calligari-Ng received his JD from Yale Law School in 2007 and his PhD in Economics from Princeton University in 2012. Before joining Berkeley in 2014, he served as a senior economist in the Antitrust Division of the U.S. Department of Justice and clerked for Judge Stephen Reinhardt on the Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals.

"Bart Calligari-Ng is the rare antitrust scholar whose work is read with equal seriousness by economists, doctrinal scholars, and enforcement officials," said Professor Reinaldo Quintanilla-Stoyanov, chair of the antitrust search committee. "His decision to join us represents a significant strengthening of our program in regulation and political economy."

The Voorhees Chair was established in 1987 by the late Henry W. Voorhees, LL.B. 1952, and his wife Joan, and has previously been held by Professors Edmund S. Ferentino (1987–2003) and Lavonne Pidgeon-Mathers (2004–2022).

---

#### Professor Saoirse Murchadh-Renwick — Professor of Law

Professor Murchadh-Renwick, who joins Columbia Law School from the University of Chicago Law School, is a constitutional law scholar whose work bridges originalism and democratic theory. Her current book project, *The Senate and the Constitution: A Structural History*, is under contract with Princeton University Press.

She received her JD from Stanford Law School in 2010 and an MPhil in Politics from the University of Oxford (Balliol College) in 2007. She clerked for Judge Diane S. Sykes on the Seventh Circuit Court of Appeals and for Justice Sonia Sotomayor on the United States Supreme Court.

Her articles include "The Senate as a Federalism Institution" (*Yale Law Journal*, vol. 132, no. 6, 2023, with Hortense Bellweather-Onuchukwu); "Originalism and the Lower-Court Judge" (*Harvard Law Review*, vol. 134, no. 8, 2021); and "What the Reconstruction Senate Knew" (*University of Chicago Law Review*, vol. 88, no. 3, 2021).

"Professor Murchadh-Renwick is the kind of constitutional law scholar who is read across the methodological divide," said Professor Wakefield Underbrink-Yoshioka, chair of the constitutional law search committee. "Her work will substantially strengthen our public-law offerings, and our students will benefit enormously from her arrival in the classroom."

---

#### Professor Hiroshi Akinwumi-Delacroix — Associate Professor of Law

Professor Akinwumi-Delacroix, who joins Columbia Law School from NYU School of Law, is an international tax scholar whose work focuses on the institutional design of multilateral tax instruments, on the OECD BEPS framework, and on the political economy of tax cooperation among developing economies.

He received his JD from Harvard Law School in 2013 and his LL.M. in Taxation from NYU in 2014, and is a graduate (with first-class honours) of the University of Lagos. Before entering academia, he practiced international tax at Skadden, Arps, Slate, Meagher & Flom LLP in New York and at Mori Hamada & Matsumoto in Tokyo.

His recent publications include "The Two-Pillar Solution: A Critical Reconstruction" (*Tax Law Review*, vol. 76, no. 2, 2023); "Tax Cooperation and the Global South" (*Columbia Journal of Transnational Law*, vol. 60, no. 4, 2022); and "Treaty Shopping Revisited" (*Florida Tax Review*, vol. 25, no. 1, 2021).

---

#### Professor Ndidi Rosalind Babatunde-Cariou — Patricia Williams Professor of Law

Professor Babatunde-Cariou, who joins Columbia Law School from Georgetown Law as the inaugural Patricia Williams Professor of Law, is a scholar of critical race theory, civil procedure, and the history of American legal thought. Her award-winning book *The Color of Procedure: Race and Civil Justice in the Twentieth Century* (Harvard University Press, 2022) received the 2023 Order of the Coif Book Award and the 2023 American Society for Legal History Cromwell Book Prize.

She received her JD from Yale Law School in 2008 and her PhD in History from Princeton University in 2014. She clerked for Judge Damon Keith on the Sixth Circuit Court of Appeals and served as Special Assistant to the Assistant Attorney General for Civil Rights, U.S. Department of Justice, from 2009 to 2010.

"It is a particular honor to welcome Professor Babatunde-Cariou as the inaugural holder of the Patricia Williams Chair," said Dean Vasquez-Brindisi. "Her scholarship continues, deepens, and complicates the intellectual project to which Professor Williams gave her career."

The Patricia Williams Professorship was established in 2022 through a $7.5 million gift from Reuben and Sigrid Habermehl-Brzezinski (LL.B. 1968) honoring the late Professor Patricia J. Williams, who taught at Columbia Law School from 1991 to 2020 and was the James L. Dohr Professor of Law at the time of her retirement.

### Designation of Two Existing Faculty to Named Chairs

#### Professor Caleb Wenzhou Halberstam — Michael I. Sovern Professor of Law

Professor Halberstam, who joined the Columbia Law faculty in 2014, has been designated the Michael I. Sovern Professor of Law, succeeding the late Professor Lance Liebman, who held the chair from 1991 until his death in 2020 and after whom Vermont Law School has named its visiting-scholars program in employment law.

Professor Halberstam is a scholar of administrative law and federal courts. His book *The Administrative State's Constitution* (Yale University Press, 2021) was awarded the 2022 American Bar Association Silver Gavel Award and the 2022 Robert L. Stern Award. He received his JD from Yale Law School in 2003 and clerked for Judge Stephen Breyer on the First Circuit and for Justice Ruth Bader Ginsburg on the United States Supreme Court.

The Michael I. Sovern Chair was established in 2002 by friends and colleagues of the late President Emeritus Michael I. Sovern, LL.B. 1955.

#### Professor Marisela Quiñones-Eastman — Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Professor of Law

Professor Quiñones-Eastman, who joined the Columbia Law faculty in 2008, has been designated the Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Professor of Law. A scholar of immigration law, constitutional law, and human rights, she received her JD from Yale Law School in 1998 and her DPhil in Politics from the University of Oxford (Nuffield College) in 2002.

Her recent works include *The Borders of Citizenship* (Harvard University Press, 2020); "Plyler at Forty" (*Columbia Law Review*, vol. 122, no. 5, 2022); and the casebook *Immigration and Citizenship: Process and Policy* (with Aleinikoff, Martin, and Motomura, West Academic, 9th ed. 2021).

The Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Chair was established in 1986 by the family of Isidor Sulzbacher, LL.B. 1929, and has previously been held by Professors Henri DeWitt-Vasilescu (1986–2004) and Margery Ashmolean-Quinn (2005–2022).

### Backgrounder: The Search Process

The four faculty appointments announced today are the result of search processes that, in each case, were initiated by faculty resolution between September 2021 and April 2022 and were chaired in each case by a senior tenured member of the Columbia Law faculty. The composition of each search committee, and the search committee's report to the faculty, are matters of internal faculty governance. The following committee chairs may, however, be identified:

- **Antitrust and Industrial Organization search:** Chaired by Professor Reinaldo Quintanilla-Stoyanov.
- **Constitutional Law search:** Chaired by Professor Wakefield Underbrink-Yoshioka.
- **International Tax search:** Chaired by Professor Margery Halberstrom-Cavendish.
- **Critical Race Theory and Civil Procedure search:** Chaired by Professor Kwabena Dansoman-Ferreira.

The two endowed chair designations, by contrast, do not result from formal faculty searches and are made by the Dean in consultation with the relevant standing faculty committees. The designations announced today were approved by the Faculty Appointments Committee at its meeting of February 24, 2023.

### Backgrounder: The Patricia Williams Chair

The Patricia Williams Professorship of Law was established at Columbia Law School in 2022 through a $7.5 million gift from the alumni couple Reuben Habermehl-Brzezinski (Columbia Law LL.B. 1968) and Sigrid Habermehl-Brzezinski. The gift, announced at the Law School's June 2022 reunion, is the largest single endowed-chair gift in the Law School's history.

The chair honors the late Professor Patricia J. Williams, who served on the Columbia Law faculty from 1991 to 2020 and held the James L. Dohr Professorship from 2010 until her retirement. Professor Williams is the author of, among other works, *The Alchemy of Race and Rights* (Harvard University Press, 1991), *The Rooster's Egg: On the Persistence of Prejudice* (Harvard University Press, 1995), and *Seeing a Color-Blind Future: The Paradox of Race* (Farrar, Straus and Giroux, 1997). She was a MacArthur Fellow (2000) and a recipient of the American Bar Association's Margaret Brent Women Lawyers of Achievement Award (2008).

The Habermehl-Brzezinski gift establishes, in addition to the chair, an annual public lecture in Professor Williams's honor and a graduate fellowship program supporting JSD students working in the broad area of race and the law.

### Backgrounder: The Michael I. Sovern Chair

The Michael I. Sovern Professorship of Law was established at Columbia Law School in 2002 through a fund-raising campaign organized by alumni of Columbia Law School graduating between 1955 and 1985, in honor of Michael I. Sovern (1931–2020), who served as President of Columbia University from 1980 to 1993 and as Dean of Columbia Law School from 1970 to 1979.

President Sovern was, before his administrative service, a scholar of labor law, administrative law, and dispute resolution. He held the Chancellor Kent Professorship and, after his return to the faculty in 1993, the Sovern Professorship (the latter having been redesignated in his honor at the conclusion of his presidency). The chair created in 2002 — the Michael I. Sovern Professorship of Law — is distinct from the latter designation and was held from its establishment in 2002 until 2020 by the late Professor Lance Liebman.

Professor Liebman, the former Dean of Columbia Law School (1991–1996) and Director of the American Law Institute (1999–2014), held the Sovern Chair from 2002 until his death on November 4, 2020. The designation of Professor Halberstam to the chair, announced today, is the first designation since Professor Liebman's death.

### Backgrounder: The Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Chair

The Isidor and Seville Sulzbacher Professorship of Law was established at Columbia Law School in 1986 by the family of Isidor Sulzbacher, LL.B. 1929, and his wife Seville, in recognition of Isidor Sulzbacher's distinguished career at the New York commercial law firm of Sulzbacher, Goldsmith & Marcus (now part of a successor firm) and of the family's longstanding support of the Law School.

The chair has previously been held by Professors Henri DeWitt-Vasilescu (1986–2004) and Margery Ashmolean-Quinn (2005–2022). Professor Ashmolean-Quinn, the former Vice Dean for Curriculum, retired from the Columbia Law faculty in May 2022.

### Backgrounder: The Voorhees Chair

The Henry W. and Joan T. Voorhees Professorship of Law was established at Columbia Law School in 1987 through a $4.2 million gift from Mr. Henry W. Voorhees, LL.B. 1952, and his wife Joan. Mr. Voorhees, who died in 2009, was a senior partner at the New York law firm of Voorhees, Lemkin & Mansfield and served as President of the New York City Bar Association from 1987 to 1989. The chair has previously been held by Professors Edmund S. Ferentino (1987–2003) and Lavonne Pidgeon-Mathers (2004–2022).

### Backgrounder: 2023–2024 Faculty Composition

With the appointments announced today, the Columbia Law School faculty for the 2023–2024 academic year will comprise 130 full-time members, of whom:

- 87 are tenured Professors of Law
- 14 are tenure-track Associate Professors of Law
- 11 are Clinical Professors of Law
- 8 are Lecturers in Law
- 10 are Professors of Practice

Female faculty constitute 47.7% of the full-time faculty, an increase of approximately three percentage points over five years ago. Faculty of color constitute 39.2% of the full-time faculty, an increase of approximately seven percentage points over five years ago.

The full faculty roster is published annually in the Law School's *Bulletin of Information* and is available on the Law School's website.

### A Note on the Dean's Faculty Hiring Initiative

The appointments announced today are the final cohort to be hired under the Dean's Faculty Hiring Initiative, an institutional program launched at the start of Dean Vasquez-Brindisi's deanship in July 2020 with the stated objective of recruiting a total of fifteen new tenured or tenure-track faculty members over a three-year period. With today's announcement, the Initiative has met its numerical goal.

The Dean's Office is in the early stages of planning a successor initiative for the deanship's second term, the principal contours of which will be presented to the faculty at the September 2023 faculty retreat.

### Backgrounder: A Note on the Dean's Curricular Initiatives

The faculty appointments announced today are intended to support several of the Dean's curricular initiatives, including:

- **The Initiative on Race, Law, and Society** — established 2021. The Initiative supports the development of an integrated curriculum in race and the law spanning the first-year Civil Procedure course, an upper-level Critical Race Theory survey, and a series of advanced seminars and workshops. Professor Babatunde-Cariou will serve as Faculty Director of the Initiative.
- **The Initiative on Regulation and Political Economy** — established 2020. The Initiative supports interdisciplinary teaching and research at the intersection of administrative law, antitrust, financial regulation, and political economy. Professor Calligari-Ng will join the Faculty Steering Committee of the Initiative.
- **The Initiative on Constitutional Democracy** — established 2022. The Initiative supports curricular and scholarly engagement with questions of constitutional design, democratic institutions, and the rule of law. Professor Murchadh-Renwick will serve as Co-Director of the Initiative alongside Professor Underbrink-Yoshioka.
- **The Initiative on International Tax** — established 2019. The Initiative supports the Law School's leading position in international tax scholarship and teaching. Professor Akinwumi-Delacroix will join the Faculty Steering Committee.

### A Concluding Note from the Dean

"The Columbia Law School we are building is a school for the next generation of legal practice and legal scholarship — a school that takes seriously both the deep traditions of doctrinal rigor that have always defined this institution and the urgent, contemporary questions that the law is now asked to address," Dean Vasquez-Brindisi said in remarks to the faculty earlier this week.

"The colleagues we are welcoming today, and the colleagues we are recognizing today with these chair designations, represent that synthesis. They are scholars of the first rank, teachers of demonstrated excellence, and intellectual leaders whose work will define the conversations in their fields for the next several decades. The faculty's enthusiastic embrace of their candidacies is, I think, the clearest possible signal that the school's intellectual culture is in robust health."

The Dean's full remarks to the faculty are available on the Law School's internal faculty website. The Dean will deliver a public lecture on the state of the Law School at the September 2023 Convocation; the lecture will be open to alumni and members of the public and will be live-streamed via the Law School's website.
