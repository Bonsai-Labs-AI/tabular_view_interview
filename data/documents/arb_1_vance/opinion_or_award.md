## I. Introduction and Procedural Posture

1. This Partial Final Award addresses liability arising out of disputes under a Stock Purchase Agreement dated 3 March 2018 (the "SPA"), pursuant to which Claimant Meridian Capital Partners, L.P. ("Meridian") acquired one hundred percent (100%) of the issued and outstanding equity interests of Arden Energy Subholdco II, LLC from Respondents (collectively, "Arden") for a base purchase price of USD 612,000,000, subject to customary adjustments and an earn-out of up to USD 95,000,000 tied to 2019–2020 EBITDA milestones (the "Transaction").

2. By Request for Arbitration dated 22 January 2020, Meridian asserted claims for (i) breach of the representations and warranties set forth in Article IV of the SPA, in particular Sections 4.07 (Financial Statements), 4.11 (Absence of Undisclosed Liabilities), and 4.18 (Environmental Matters); (ii) fraudulent inducement; and (iii) indemnification under Article IX. Arden filed its Answer and Counterclaim on 6 March 2020, asserting that Meridian had improperly withheld earn-out payments due in respect of fiscal year 2019.

3. The Tribunal was constituted on 14 May 2020. The Terms of Reference were signed by the Parties and the Sole Arbitrator on 31 July 2020. A two-week evidentiary hearing was conducted in New York from 8 March 2021 through 19 March 2021, with closing submissions filed on 28 May 2021.

## II. Applicable Law

4. Section 10.07 of the SPA provides that the agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to its conflict-of-laws principles. The Parties do not dispute the application of Delaware substantive law to the merits, nor the application of the Federal Arbitration Act, 9 U.S.C. § 1 *et seq.*, and Article 21 of the ICC Rules (2017) to procedural questions.

## III. Findings on the Section 4.07 Claim

5. Meridian contends that the audited financial statements for fiscal year 2017, attached as Schedule 4.07(a) to the SPA, materially overstated EBITDA by approximately USD 23.4 million through the improper capitalization of recurring workover expenses at the Permian Basin assets. The Tribunal finds, on the basis of the expert report of Mr. Halvorsen and the contemporaneous email traffic admitted as Exhibits C-188 through C-212, that the capitalization practice was inconsistent with the Company's stated accounting policies and with GAAP as articulated in ASC 360-10-25.

6. The Delaware Supreme Court has held that a buyer asserting a breach of a financial-statement representation must establish (i) the existence of the representation, (ii) its falsity at the relevant date, and (iii) loss. *See Cobalt Operating, LLC v. James Crystal Enters., LLC*, 2007 WL 2142926, at \*27 (Del. Ch. July 20, 2007); *see also Akorn, Inc. v. Fresenius Kabi AG*, 2018 WL 4719347 (Del. Ch. Oct. 1, 2018). Each element is satisfied here.

7. The Tribunal accordingly finds Arden liable for breach of Section 4.07 of the SPA. Quantum shall be determined in a subsequent phase pursuant to Procedural Order No. 7.

## IV. Disposition

8. For the reasons above, the Tribunal **FINDS** Respondents liable on the Section 4.07 claim and **DEFERS** all remaining claims and counterclaims to the quantum phase.
