# Securities Arbitration Commentator

**Vol. XLI, Issue 4 | April 2023**

## Whitfield Issues $3.25M Award Against Halberg in Structured-Notes Dispute

*By Caroline Estevez, Staff Reporter*

A FINRA arbitration panel chaired by veteran neutral **James R. Whitfield** has ordered Halberg Securities Inc. and one of its registered representatives to pay $3.25 million to a New York family office over a portfolio of autocallable structured notes that the claimant said were sold to it as conservative income products.

The award, issued March 14 in *Meridian Capital Partners LP v. Halberg Securities Inc.*, FINRA No. 22-01847, is among the larger single-claimant structured-products awards on the FINRA docket this quarter, according to data published by the Securities Arbitration Commentator. Notably, Whitfield denied Meridian's request for punitive damages and attorneys' fees, a decision that several practitioners characterized as consistent with his reputation for "tightly reasoned, business-like" awards.

Whitfield, 58, has been a fixture of the New York securities bar for three decades. He spent the bulk of his career at **Hartwell & Brennan LLP**, the white-shoe litigation boutique, where he was elevated to the partnership in 2001 and ultimately served as co-chair of the firm's broker-dealer practice. He left Hartwell in 2017 to launch Whitfield ADR Services LLC, a solo neutral practice focused on securities, M&A, and financial-services disputes.

"Jim has a particular knack for cutting through the volume of paper that institutional broker-dealer cases tend to generate," said one claimant-side attorney who has appeared before him on three occasions. "He moves cases. You will not be in front of him for two years."

That efficiency-minded approach has made Whitfield a sought-after chair on the FINRA Chairperson-Eligible roster and on the AAA's Large, Complex Case panel. He also serves on FINRA's Mediation roster and on the CPR Institute's Panel of Distinguished Neutrals, giving him three FINRA-side appointments in addition to his AAA work.

A Yale Law graduate (J.D. 1993) and Princeton economics alumnus, Whitfield has authored a number of widely cited awards in the post-2018 wave of structured-product litigation. In *Northbridge Pension Trust v. Castellan Wealth Advisors* (2021), he dismissed a $22 million fiduciary-duty claim on the merits in what counsel described as one of the more decisive defense outcomes of that year.

Reached for comment, Whitfield declined to discuss the Meridian award beyond confirming its issuance. Counsel for Halberg Securities indicated the firm "respectfully disagrees with portions of the panel's findings" but did not say whether it would seek to vacate.

Halberg has thirty days from the date of the award to satisfy the judgment under FINRA Rule 12904(j).
