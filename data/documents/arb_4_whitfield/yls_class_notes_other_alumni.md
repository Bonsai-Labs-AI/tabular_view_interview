# Yale Law Report

## Class Notes — Class of 1995

**30th Reunion Issue | Spring 2025**

---

### From Your Class Secretary

Dear classmates,

It is hard to believe that thirty years have passed since we walked
across the platform at Woolsey Hall.

This reunion issue gathers updates from those of you who responded to my
(admittedly insistent) outreach over the past several months, organized
loosely by current geography.

As always, the omissions are mine, not yours — if your update did not
make it into this issue, please send it along for the next.

The reunion itself is scheduled for the weekend of October 17-19, 2025,
with the class dinner on Saturday evening at Mory's.

Margaret Quinones-Hu (your reunion co-chair, alongside David
Bezuidenhout) reports that registration is running well ahead of the
corresponding pace at our 25th, which she takes as a hopeful sign.

I look forward to seeing as many of you as can make it.

With warm regards,

Esperanza Caraballo
Class Secretary, YLS '95

---

### Northeast

**Margaret Quinones-Hu** has been appointed as the next Dean of the
Cardozo School of Law at Yeshiva University, effective July 1, 2025.

Margaret has spent the last sixteen years at Fordham Law, where she most
recently served as Vice Dean and Professor of Law.

Her scholarship focuses on transnational commercial law and
choice-of-forum clauses.

Margaret writes that she is "terrified and delighted in roughly equal
measure."

**David Bezuidenhout** retired from his partnership at
Cravath, Swaine & Moore at the end of 2024 after twenty-six years at
the firm.

David served as the head of Cravath's litigation department from 2018
to 2023.

He and his husband, the architect Saif Mehdi-Bezuidenhout, are spending
the year traveling, with extended stays planned in Cape Town, Lisbon,
and Kyoto.

David writes: "I have not opened my Outlook in eleven weeks. It feels
like recovering from a long illness."

**Hugh Tarbet-Pomeroy** has been named Chief Legal Officer of
Vendra Health, a digital-health unicorn that went public in 2024.

Hugh joins Vendra from a sixteen-year run as General Counsel of
Glanville Pharmaceuticals.

He continues to reside in Bronxville with his wife, the pediatrician
Maeve Tarbet-Pomeroy, and their three children.

**Caroline Pirjevec** has been appointed by Governor Hochul to the
New York State Court of Claims.

Caroline previously served as Deputy Counsel to the Governor and, before
that, as a partner at Paul Weiss.

She writes that her two oldest are now both at Yale College and that she
has "officially run out of children to apply to Yale," which she
characterizes as a personal milestone.

**Annapurna Devarakonda** continues her practice at Sullivan & Cromwell,
where she is now the co-head of the firm's white-collar defense group.

Annapurna writes that her oldest, Vikram, is a freshman at Yale College,
"which has occasioned more reflection than I would have predicted."

**Frederick Brattle-Owens** has joined the Vermont Law School faculty as
the Professor of Environmental Law, after thirteen years in private
practice at Earthjustice.

Freddie is also the author of *Reservoirs and Reckonings: Western Water
Law in a Warming Climate* (University of California Press, 2024).

He writes that South Royalton is "almost too quiet" but that he is
adjusting.

**Yolanda Steyn** has been promoted to General Counsel of the
New York Public Library, where she has worked since 2017.

Yolanda was previously Deputy General Counsel and, before that, a
partner at Patterson Belknap.

**Frederick Owusu-Tang** continues to practice as a partner at
Wachtell, Lipton, Rosen & Katz, where he leads the firm's antitrust
practice.

Freddie also serves on the Council of the American Law Institute.

**Sienna Greaves-Ashworth** has joined the faculty of NYU School of
Law as the Howard E. Dolinger Professor of Law, after seventeen years
on the faculty of Columbia.

Sienna's scholarship focuses on bankruptcy and corporate reorganization.

She and her wife, the painter Persephone Greaves, have relocated from
Morningside Heights to the West Village.

**Olamiposi Onatade** has been promoted to Senior Vice President and
General Counsel of MetLife Investment Management, based in Whippany,
New Jersey.

### Mid-Atlantic and Washington

**Theresa Almodovar** was confirmed by the Senate in January 2025 as a
Judge of the U.S. District Court for the Eastern District of Pennsylvania.

Theresa most recently served as the U.S. Attorney for the Eastern
District.

Several classmates attended her investiture in Philadelphia in
February.

**Wendell Christy Yarboro** has been appointed Deputy Solicitor General
of the United States.

Wendell previously argued seventeen cases in the Supreme Court and
reports, with characteristic dryness, that "the eighteenth will be more
interesting than the seventeenth."

**Constance Achterberg** has been named the inaugural holder of the
Howard L. Aubrey Chair in Constitutional Law at Georgetown University
Law Center.

Connie's most recent book, *The Reluctant Majority: How the Modern
Supreme Court Decides Whom Not to Reverse*, won the 2024 ABA Silver
Gavel Award.

**Marquis A. Pendleton** continues his practice at Pendleton Reston
PLLC, the Washington appellate boutique he founded in 2011.

Marquis argued *Henning v. Department of the Interior* in the Supreme
Court in March 2025.

**Sookie Jang-Beltran** has been promoted to Senior Vice President and
Chief Compliance Officer of Capital One Financial Corporation, based in
McLean, Virginia.

**Pavlos Drakopoulos** writes from his sailboat, currently moored in
Annapolis: "After twenty-eight years at the Department of Justice, I
retired in November. The boat is the next chapter. We'll see."

**Heliodora Vargas-Kim** has joined the faculty of the Antonin Scalia
Law School at George Mason University as the inaugural Glassberg
Distinguished Professor of Administrative Law.

Heli's most recent book, *The Reasoned Explanation Doctrine*, is
forthcoming from Cambridge University Press.

**Cassius Tewelde-Marchetti** has been promoted to General Counsel of
the World Bank Group's International Finance Corporation, based in
Washington.

Cass writes that he and his wife, Isabela, are downsizing from their
Bethesda house now that their three children have all left for college.

**Cyprian Foxworth-Renteria** continues as a partner at Williams &
Connolly, where he co-chairs the firm's appellate practice.

Cy argued two cases in the Supreme Court during the October 2024 term.

### South and Southeast

**Letitia Hawksworth** has been elected to a second term as the
Attorney General of North Carolina.

Letitia is also serving as the President of the National Association of
Attorneys General for the 2024-2025 cycle.

**Calvin Mendez-Ortiz** has joined the faculty of Duke Law School as
the Charles L. Crowell Professor of Law, after fifteen years on the
faculty of UNC Law.

Calvin's scholarship focuses on antitrust law and platform regulation.

**Anita Belorussova-Frey** continues her practice at Holland & Knight in
Miami, where she chairs the firm's Latin America practice.

Anita writes that she is "trying, mostly unsuccessfully, to spend less
time on airplanes."

**Octavia Renfrew-Hines** has been appointed by Governor Cooper as the
Chief Administrative Judge of the North Carolina Court of Appeals.

**Donovan Cleary-Worth** opened a solo criminal-defense practice in
Charleston, South Carolina, in 2023 after a twenty-year career as a
federal public defender.

He writes: "I had forgotten that lawyers have to do their own copying.
It has been instructive."

**Tabitha Esposito-Walmsley** has been named Chief Compliance Officer
of NextEra Energy, based in Juno Beach, Florida.

Tabby was previously Deputy General Counsel of Duke Energy.

**Hippolyte Sanon-Beaumont** has been promoted to General Counsel of
the Tennessee Valley Authority, based in Knoxville.

**Reinaldo Ocampo-Sterling** continues as the elected District Attorney
for the Eleventh Judicial Circuit (Miami-Dade County), Florida.

He was re-elected to a fourth term in November 2024.

### Midwest

**Phineas Talleyrand** has been named the inaugural Dean of the new
Western Lakes School of Law at the University of Wisconsin-Eau Claire.

The school will admit its first class in the fall of 2026.

**Karenina Bjornsdottir** continues to serve as a Justice of the
Minnesota Supreme Court, to which she was appointed in 2019.

Her term runs through 2031.

**Edmondo Ricci** has been promoted to Group General Counsel of
Caterpillar Inc., based in Peoria.

Ed and his wife, Theresa, will be relocating from Chicago in the summer.

**Penelope Aroyan** has joined the faculty of the University of Chicago
Law School as the Walter F. Murphy Distinguished Professor of Law,
after twelve years on the faculty of Northwestern.

Penny's most recent book, *Insurgent Constitutionalism*, is forthcoming
from Harvard University Press in 2026.

**Beatrice Vouvray-McKim** has been named Chief Diversity Officer at
Kirkland & Ellis.

Bea continues to practice as a partner in the firm's litigation group.

**Octavian Lassiter-Hayes** continues his solo appellate practice in
Indianapolis, where he argued and won three Indiana Supreme Court
cases in 2024.

**Mei-Lin Quartararo-Dawes** has been appointed to the Iowa Supreme
Court by Governor Reynolds.

Mei-Lin previously served as a partner at Belin McCormick in
Des Moines.

**Cesare Burroughs-Halberstam** has joined the faculty of the
University of Michigan Law School as a Clinical Professor, after
fourteen years as the head of the public-interest practice at

### West Coast

**Hisao Chimoto-Velez** has been appointed by Governor Newsom to the
California Supreme Court.

Hisao is the first justice on the court to have been born in Okinawa.

**Imogen Sutterfield-Knox** has been elected as the District Attorney
of Alameda County, California.

Imogen previously served as the elected District Attorney of
San Mateo County.

**Casper Naeemi-Holzwarth** continues his practice at Wilson Sonsini in
Palo Alto, where he co-chairs the firm's venture-capital practice.

Casper writes that he has "watched the AI sector go from theoretical to
overheated to apparently terminal to suddenly central, all in a single
four-year stretch, and I have stopped pretending to predict anything."

**Larisse Bonpensiero-Tashjian** has joined the Ninth Circuit Court of
Appeals as a senior staff attorney.

She had previously been a federal magistrate judge for the Northern
District of California.

**Tobias O'Halloran-Mendel** retired from his litigation practice at
Munger, Tolles & Olson in early 2025 and has accepted a one-year
fellowship at the Stanford Center for Internet and Society.

Toby writes: "I find I am still interested in how the internet works.
I am told this dates me."

**Sophronia Bell-Caulfield** has been promoted to General Counsel of
Twilio, based in San Francisco.

Soph was previously Deputy General Counsel of Slack Technologies before
its acquisition by Salesforce.

**Pulkit Vajpayee-Stern** has joined the faculty of UC Berkeley Law
as the inaugural Edmond J. Safra Professor of Tax Law, after fifteen
years on the faculty of USC Gould.

**Aldebaran Wozniak-Pearce** continues as a partner at Cooley LLP in
Palo Alto, where he co-chairs the firm's emerging-companies practice.

Al writes that he is "extremely tired" but otherwise well.

**Querida Saavedra-Tibbett** has been promoted to General Counsel of
Stripe, based in South San Francisco.

### In Memoriam

The class regrets to report the passing of three classmates since our
25th reunion:

- **Henrietta DuBois Lavanchy** (1969-2023) of New Orleans, Louisiana,
  who served as the General Counsel of Tulane University and as the
  founding chair of the Louisiana Center for Civil Rights Litigation.
  Henrietta passed in November 2023 after a brief illness.
  The class will dedicate a bench in her memory in the Sterling Law
  Building courtyard.

- **Reynaldo Castellanos-Penzel** (1968-2024) of Madrid, Spain,
  who served for many years as General Counsel of Banco Solana
  International and who was a beloved fixture of the class's irregular
  European gatherings.
  Rey passed in May 2024.

- **Margaret Saito-Yeager** (1969-2024) of Seattle, Washington,
  who served as a partner at Perkins Coie and as the chair of the
  King County Bar Foundation.
  Maggie passed in September 2024.

A memorial reception will be held during reunion weekend on the Friday
evening, October 17, 2025.

Family members of the deceased are welcome and will be the guests of
the class.

### Class Notes Editor's Postscript

Several classmates wrote in with updates that were too personal to
include in detail but that I want to acknowledge in the aggregate.

Six classmates report that they have become grandparents for the first
time since our last update.

Four classmates report that they have completed their first marathons,
two of them in their fifties.

Three classmates report that they have left long-term firm partnerships
to teach, write, or take up entirely different second careers.

Several others wrote in with updates that were more difficult — health
challenges, the loss of parents, divorces, the long process of
re-imagining what the next two decades of life might look like.

I am grateful to all of you who shared.
