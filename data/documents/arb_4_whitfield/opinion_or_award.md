### CASE INFORMATION

Statement of Claim filed on or about February 9, 2022. Claimant asserted causes of action for (i) violation of FINRA Conduct Rule 2111 (suitability); (ii) breach of fiduciary duty; (iii) negligent misrepresentation; and (iv) breach of contract, arising out of the recommendation and sale of approximately $14.7 million in autocallable structured notes referenced to a basket of mid-cap regional bank equities between June 2019 and November 2020.

Respondents filed their Statement of Answer on April 4, 2022, denying the substantive allegations and asserting affirmative defenses of ratification, failure to mitigate, and contributory fault.

### CASE SUMMARY

Claimant Meridian Capital Partners LP is a New York-based family-office investment vehicle. Respondent Halberg Securities Inc. is a FINRA-registered broker-dealer headquartered in Jersey City, New Jersey. Respondent Voss was, at all relevant times, a registered representative of Halberg and Claimant's primary point of contact.

Claimant alleged that the structured notes at issue were materially mismatched to its stated investment objective of "capital preservation with modest income," and that Respondents failed to disclose the principal-at-risk features and correlation risk embedded in the underlying basket. Respondents contended that Claimant's principal, Mr. Avi Reinholt, was a sophisticated investor who had executed risk-acknowledgement documentation on three separate occasions.

### AWARD

After due consideration of the pleadings, the testimony and evidence presented at the evidentiary hearing held over six sessions between January 30 and February 8, 2023, and the post-hearing submissions of the parties, the undersigned Arbitrator finds as follows:

1. Respondents Halberg Securities Inc. and Daniel R. Voss are jointly and severally liable to Claimant Meridian Capital Partners LP for compensatory damages in the amount of **$3,250,000.00**;

2. Claimant's claim for punitive damages is denied;

3. Claimant's claim for attorneys' fees is denied, the panel finding no statutory or contractual basis for such an award;

4. Pre-award interest is awarded at the New York statutory rate of 9% per annum from December 1, 2020, through the date of this Award;

5. The FINRA filing fees and forum fees shall be borne equally by the parties as previously assessed;

6. Any and all relief not specifically addressed herein, including Respondents' counterclaim for indemnification, is **DENIED**.

This Award is in full and final resolution of the issues submitted for determination.
